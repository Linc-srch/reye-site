Measuring progress toward AGI: A cognitive framework

Measuring progress toward AGI: A cognitive framework

2026-05-18 · deepmind_blog

Summary

Google DeepMind introduces a cognitive framework to measure progress toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), drawing on decades of research in psychology, neuroscience, and cognitive science. The framework identifies ten key cognitive abilities—perception, generation, attention, learning, memory, reasoning, metacognition, executive functions, problem solving, and social cognition—and proposes a three-stage evaluation protocol benchmarking AI against human performance. To operationalize this, DeepMind launches a Kaggle hackathon with a $200,000 prize pool, inviting the community to design evaluations for five abilities where gaps are largest: learning, metacognition, attention, executive functions, and social cognition.

摘要

Google DeepMind 提出一个基于认知科学的框架来衡量AGI进展，该框架识别了十项关键认知能力，包括perception、generation、attention、learning、memory、reasoning、metacognition、executive functions、problem solving和social cognition，并设计了将AI性能与人类基线对比的三阶段评估协议。为将理论付诸实践，DeepMind在Kaggle上发起奖金20万美元的黑客松，邀请社区针对learning、metacognition、attention、executive functions和social cognition这五个评估缺口最大的能力设计评测。
