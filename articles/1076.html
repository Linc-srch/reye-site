2) CRGP

C — Context

Federated fine-tuning of LLMs suffers from statistical heterogeneity (non-IID data) causing client drift and degraded global model performance. Existing defenses like FedProx, SCAFFOLD, and FedDPA operate at the model level, constraining weight updates or separating adapters, but overlook the root cause of intrinsic data distribution mismatches. Self-distillation has shown success in centralized LLM training for improving instruction quality, but its potential for aligning heterogeneous data distributions in federated settings remains underexplored.

R — Related work

  • FedProx (Li et al., 2020) and SCAFFOLD (Karimireddy et al., 2020) constrain local updates to reduce client drift.
  • FedDPA (Yang et al., 2024a) uses dual LoRA to separate global and personalized adapters.
  • Knowledge distillation in FL (FedGen, FedDistill) transfers knowledge via logits but often requires public proxy datasets.
  • Self-distillation in centralized LLM training (WizardLM, Alpaca) refines training data to improve instruction quality.
  • Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) techniques, especially LoRA (Hu et al., 2022), are standard for federated LLM fine-tuning.

G — Gap

Existing model-level defenses for federated LLM fine-tuning treat symptoms (weight divergence) rather than the root cause: intrinsic data distribution mismatches across clients. While self-distillation has been used in centralized settings, its application to align heterogeneous client data distributions in federated learning is unexplored, and naive self-distillation can inadvertently increase hallucinations and stylistic redundancy (the 'Rewrite Paradox').

P — Proposal

FedSDR introduces a data-centric paradigm: first, Federated Self-Distillation (FedSD) projects client raw data into a smoothed 'Model Understanding Space' via self-distillation, aligning heterogeneous distributions. To counter the Rewrite Paradox, FedSDR adds a dual-stream mechanism: a local LoRA-S (Smoothing) branch absorbs distributional noise from distilled data, and a parallel global LoRA-R (Rectification) branch learns factual correctness from raw data. Only LoRA-R is aggregated globally, yielding a faithful and aligned model.

4) Experiments

Main results

FedSDR achieves state-of-the-art performance across various Non-IID regimes; for example, on the Databricks Dolly-15K dataset, t-SNE visualization confirms that self-distillation dissolves distinct client clusters into a shared distribution. Detailed numerical results (e.g., accuracy, perplexity) are referenced in Tables 1 and 2 of the paper, showing superior performance over FedAvg, FedProx, and FedDPA baselines.

Ablation

The dual-stream design is critical: selectively aggregating only LoRA-R (rectification) filters out noise and hallucinations absorbed by LoRA-S, while LoRA-S alone (without rectification) suffers from the Rewrite Paradox. The paper shows that implicit smoothing from self-distilled data in LoRA-S eases raw-data learning in LoRA-R, highlighting the synergy.

Limitations

['The approach relies on self-distillation which may require additional computation on each client for data rewriting.', 'The dual-stream LoRA mechanism increases memory and communication overhead compared to single-adapter methods.', 'The paper does not extensively evaluate on very large-scale federated settings (e.g., 1000+ clients) or under strict differential privacy constraints.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Shifts focus from model-centric to data-centric heterogeneity mitigation, which could inspire new approaches for other FL domains.
  2. Identifies and resolves the 'Rewrite Paradox', a subtle but important failure mode of self-distillation that is relevant to any distillation-based alignment method.
  3. The dual-stream LoRA design offers a clean separation of distribution smoothing and factual rectification, potentially applicable to other PEFT-based FL systems.
  4. Demonstrates that self-distillation can serve as a universal booster for existing FL algorithms, not just a standalone method.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Evaluate FedSDR under differential privacy constraints to assess privacy-utility trade-offs.
  • [ ] Test the dual-stream mechanism on other modalities (e.g., vision) or with different PEFT methods (e.g., Adapters, Prefix Tuning).
  • [ ] Explore adaptive weighting between LoRA-S and LoRA-R updates to further balance smoothing and fidelity.
  • [ ] Investigate the scalability of FedSDR to very large numbers of clients and heterogeneous hardware settings.
