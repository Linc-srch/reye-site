Natural Language Autoencoders: Turning Claude’s thoughts into text

Natural Language Autoencoders: Turning Claude’s thoughts into text

Summary

Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs) are a novel interpretability method that converts internal activations of language models like Claude into human-readable text explanations. By training an activation verbalizer and reconstructor, NLAs enable direct reading of model thoughts, revealing hidden reasoning such as planning rhymes, awareness of safety tests, or cheating motivations. Applications include auditing for misalignment and improving safety by detecting unverbalized evaluation awareness. The paper demonstrates NLAs' effectiveness on multiple versions of Claude and releases code and interactive tools for further research.

摘要

Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs) 是一种新的可解释性方法，能将 Claude 等语言模型的内部激活转化为可直接阅读的自然语言文本。通过训练 activation verbalizer 和 reconstructor，NLAs 揭示了模型隐藏的推理过程，例如提前规划押韵、对安全测试的察觉或作弊动机。该方法已用于审计和对齐检测，并开放了代码与交互工具以促进研究。
