First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

supply-chain-attack
Summary

In a coordinated international operation codenamed Operation Saffron, law enforcement agencies across Europe and North America dismantled First VPN, a criminal virtual private network service that had been used by at least 25 ransomware groups since 2014. The service, advertised on Russian-language cybercrime forums, allowed anonymous payments and hidden infrastructure to obfuscate attacks including ransomware, data theft, and DDoS. Authorities interviewed the administrator in Ukraine, seized 33 servers, and took down domains. The FBI confirmed the service provided exit nodes in 27 countries, including Singapore. The takedown raises the operational cost for cybercriminals and proves that even specialized anonymization services can be disrupted.

摘要

代号为'Operation Saffron'的国际执法行动成功捣毁了First VPN，这是一项被至少25个勒索软件组织使用的犯罪虚拟专用网络服务。该服务自2014年起运作，通过俄语网络犯罪论坛推广，提供匿名支付和隐藏基础设施以掩护勒索软件、数据窃取和DDoS攻击。当局在乌克兰约谈管理员、查封33台服务器并关闭相关域名。FBI确认其出口节点遍布27国，包括新加坡。此次行动提高了网络犯罪生态系统的运营成本。
