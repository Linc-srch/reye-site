First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

supply-chain-attack
启示 · Insight

For a system security researcher focused on OS and kernel security, this takedown is a significant operational security (OpSec) event for the cybercriminal ecosystem. It demonstrates that infrastructure-level anonymity services, which often rely on compromised or bulletproof hosting, are not invulnerable to coordinated, multi-jurisdictional law enforcement action. The technical details—the use of protocols like VLESS and Reality to disguise VPN traffic as HTTPS, the 32 exit nodes across 27 countries, and the support for WireGuard and OpenConnect—provide a concrete case study of how attackers operationalize network-level evasion. The disclosure that user identities are now known to authorities is a powerful deterrent, raising the bar for future criminal VPN operators who must now consider the risk of their infrastructure being seized and their user database compromised.

对于专注于操作系统和内核安全的系统安全研究人员来说，此次取缔是网络犯罪生态系统中一个重要的操作安全（OpSec）事件。它表明，基础设施级的匿名服务（通常依赖于被入侵的或“打不死的”主机）并非无懈可击，无法抵御协调的、跨司法管辖区的执法行动。技术细节——使用VLESS和Reality等协议将VPN流量伪装成HTTPS、分布在27个国家的32个出口节点、以及对WireGuard和OpenConnect的支持——为攻击者如何操作网络级规避提供了一个具体的案例研究。用户身份现已为当局所知这一披露是一个强大的威慑，提高了未来犯罪VPN运营者的门槛，他们现在必须考虑其基础设施被查封和用户数据库被泄露的风险。

Summary

Law enforcement in Europe and North America dismantled First VPN, a criminal VPN service used by at least 25 ransomware groups since 2014 to anonymize attacks. The operation, codenamed Operation Saffron, involved seizing 33 servers and notifying users that their identities are now known.

摘要

欧洲和北美执法机构捣毁了First VPN，这是一项自2014年以来被至少25个勒索软件团伙用于匿名化攻击的犯罪VPN服务。代号为“藏红花行动”的此次行动涉及查封33台服务器，并通知用户其身份现已为当局所知。

Deep Note

Key Points

  • First VPN, a criminal VPN service active since 2014, was dismantled in a global operation (Operation Saffron) led by France and the Netherlands.
  • The service was used by at least 25 ransomware groups (e.g., Avaddon) for network reconnaissance and intrusion anonymization.
  • It offered 32 exit nodes in 27 countries, supported protocols like OpenConnect, WireGuard, Outline, and VLESS Reality (which disguises VPN traffic as HTTPS).
  • Payments were accepted via Bitcoin and other anonymous methods; subscriptions ranged from $2/day to $483/year.
  • Authorities seized 33 servers, interviewed the administrator in Ukraine, and notified users that their identities are now known.
  • The service advertised itself as not storing logs and not cooperating with judicial authorities, a claim proven false by the takedown.

Context & Contrast

This takedown is notable for targeting a VPN service specifically marketed to and used by ransomware groups, rather than a general-purpose VPN or a bulletproof hosting provider. Previous takedowns (e.g., of ransomware leak sites or botnet C2s) have focused on specific criminal infrastructure. This operation targets the anonymization layer itself, which is a more foundational component of the ransomware supply chain. The use of advanced protocol obfuscation (VLESS Reality) indicates a sophistication in criminal tooling that was previously less common in such services.

Why It Matters

This event is significant because it disrupts a key enabler for ransomware operations: reliable, anonymous network access. By dismantling First VPN and, critically, notifying users that their identities are known, law enforcement has created a chilling effect. It raises the operational cost and risk for ransomware affiliates who relied on this service. For system security, it highlights the importance of infrastructure-level defenses and the need for researchers to understand how attackers use VPNs, proxies, and anonymization networks to evade detection and attribution. The technical details of the protocols used (VLESS, Reality) are valuable for developing network-based detection signatures.

Critical Take

While a significant operational success, the impact may be temporary. As Bitdefender noted, new anonymization services will likely emerge to fill the demand. The claim that all user identities are known should be treated with some caution; it is a powerful psychological operation, but the completeness of the data seizure is unverified. The technical details provided (e.g., specific IPs, protocols) are useful but represent a snapshot of a now-defunct service. The long-term effect on ransomware operations remains to be seen.

What to Watch

  • Emergence of replacement criminal VPN services and their operational security (OpSec) posture.
  • Analysis of the seized user database (if made public or leaked) to understand the scale and nature of the ransomware affiliate network.
  • Technical reports detailing the specific VLESS Reality configurations used and how they can be fingerprinted at the network level.
  • Any retaliatory actions by ransomware groups or changes in their infrastructure choices (e.g., moving to decentralized or Tor-based anonymization).
  • Follow-up arrests or indictments based on the identified user identities.
深度笔记

要点

  • First VPN，一项自2014年以来活跃的犯罪VPN服务，在一次由法国和荷兰领导的全球行动（藏红花行动）中被捣毁。
  • 该服务被至少25个勒索软件团伙（例如Avaddon）用于网络侦察和入侵匿名化。
  • 它提供了分布在27个国家的32个出口节点，支持OpenConnect、WireGuard、Outline和VLESS Reality（可将VPN流量伪装成HTTPS）等协议。
  • 通过比特币和其他匿名方式接受付款；订阅费用从每天2美元到每年483美元不等。
  • 当局查封了33台服务器，在乌克兰讯问了管理员，并通知用户其身份现已为当局所知。
  • 该服务宣称不存储日志且不与司法当局合作，这一说法被此次行动证明是虚假的。

背景与对比

此次行动值得注意，因为它针对的是专门面向勒索软件团伙并为其使用的VPN服务，而非通用VPN或“打不死的”主机提供商。以往的取缔行动（例如针对勒索软件泄露站点或僵尸网络C2）侧重于特定的犯罪基础设施。而此次行动针对的是匿名化层本身，这是勒索软件供应链中一个更基础的组成部分。使用先进的协议混淆技术（VLESS Reality）表明犯罪工具化达到了以前在此类服务中不太常见的复杂程度。

重要性

此事件意义重大，因为它破坏了勒索软件行动的一个关键使能因素：可靠的匿名网络访问。通过捣毁First VPN，并且关键的是，通知用户其身份已知，执法部门产生了寒蝉效应。它提高了依赖该服务的勒索软件附属机构的运营成本和风险。对于系统安全而言，它凸显了基础设施级防御的重要性，以及研究人员需要了解攻击者如何使用VPN、代理和匿名化网络来逃避检测和归因。所用协议（VLESS, Reality）的技术细节对于开发基于网络的检测签名非常有价值。

批判性视角

虽然这是一次重大的行动成功，但其影响可能是暂时的。正如Bitdefender所指出的，新的匿名化服务很可能会出现以满足需求。关于所有用户身份已知的说法应持谨慎态度；这是一次强大的心理战，但数据扣押的完整性尚未得到验证。所提供的技术细节（例如，特定IP、协议）很有用，但仅代表一个现已停用服务的快照。对勒索软件行动的长期影响仍有待观察。

后续关注

  • 替代性犯罪VPN服务的出现及其操作安全（OpSec）姿态。
  • 对被查封用户数据库的分析（如果公开或泄露），以了解勒索软件附属网络的规模和性质。
  • 详细说明所使用的特定VLESS Reality配置以及如何在网络层面进行指纹识别的技术报告。
  • 勒索软件团伙的任何报复行动或其在基础设施选择上的变化（例如，转向去中心化或基于Tor的匿名化）。
  • 基于已识别用户身份的后续逮捕或起诉。
