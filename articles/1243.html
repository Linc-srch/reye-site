First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

First VPN Dismantled in Global Takedown Over Use by 25 Ransomware Groups

supply-chain-attack
启示 · Insight

For a system security researcher focused on OS/kernel and software supply-chain security, this takedown is significant because it removes a key infrastructure component used by ransomware groups to obfuscate command-and-control (C2) traffic and initial access vectors. The dismantling of a VPN service that supported protocols like WireGuard, OpenVPN, and VLESS/Reality (which disguises traffic as HTTPS) directly impacts the operational security of threat actors. This event provides a rare data point on the real-world infrastructure dependencies of ransomware operations, offering insights into how attackers bypass network-level controls and how law enforcement can disrupt the supply chain of cybercrime tools. The technical details about the VPN's protocols (e.g., VLESS, Reality) and its use of multiple encryption options are directly relevant to understanding attacker tradecraft and developing network-level detection signatures.

对于专注于操作系统/内核和软件供应链安全的研究人员而言，此次行动意义重大，因为它移除了勒索软件团伙用于混淆命令与控制（C2）流量和初始访问途径的关键基础设施组件。一个支持WireGuard、OpenVPN以及VLESS/Reality（可将流量伪装成HTTPS）等协议的VPN服务被捣毁，直接影响了威胁行为者的操作安全性。此事件提供了一个关于勒索软件操作真实基础设施依赖性的罕见数据点，有助于深入了解攻击者如何绕过网络级控制，以及执法部门如何破坏网络犯罪工具供应链。关于该VPN协议（例如VLESS、Reality）及其使用多种加密选项的技术细节，与理解攻击者战术和开发网络级检测签名直接相关。

Summary

International law enforcement (Operation Saffron) dismantled First VPN, a criminal VPN service used by at least 25 ransomware groups since 2014 to anonymize attacks, seizing 33 servers and identifying its users.

摘要

国际执法行动（Operation Saffron）捣毁了First VPN，这是一个自2014年以来被至少25个勒索软件团伙用于匿名化攻击的犯罪VPN服务，查获了33台服务器并识别了其用户。

Deep Note

Key Points

  • First VPN was a criminal VPN service dismantled in Operation Saffron, led by France and the Netherlands.
  • Active since 2014, it provided 32 exit nodes in 27 countries and was promoted on Russian-speaking cybercrime forums.
  • Used by at least 25 ransomware groups (e.g., Avaddon) for network reconnaissance and intrusions.
  • Supported protocols: OpenConnect, WireGuard, Outline, VLESS TCP Reality, OpenVPN ECC, L2TP/IPSec, PPtP.
  • Accepted anonymous payments (Bitcoin, Perfect Money, Webmoney) and offered subscriptions from $2/day to $483/year.
  • Authorities seized 33 servers, interviewed the administrator in Ukraine, and notified users that their identities are known.
  • Bitdefender shared data on 506 users, noting that such takedowns raise the cost of cybercriminal operations.

Context & Contrast

This is the first known takedown of a VPN service specifically marketed to and used by ransomware groups, contrasting with prior actions against bulletproof hosting providers or individual ransomware infrastructure. The operation's scope (18 countries, 33 servers) and the explicit targeting of a 'criminal VPN' as a service represent a new frontier in disrupting the ransomware supply chain. Previous efforts focused on takedowns of ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) platforms or cryptocurrency exchanges; this targets the anonymization layer that enables the entire ecosystem.

Why It Matters

This takedown demonstrates a significant operational capability by law enforcement to dismantle the infrastructure that enables ransomware attacks. For researchers, it validates the hypothesis that disrupting anonymization services can have a cascading effect on the cybercriminal ecosystem, increasing operational costs and reducing trust in such services. The technical details (protocols, payment methods, infrastructure locations) provide valuable intelligence for developing network-based detection and attribution techniques. It also highlights the importance of international cooperation in tackling cybercrime infrastructure.

Critical Take

While a significant win, the article notes that 'new anonymization services will appear.' The long-term impact depends on whether this creates a sustained deterrent effect or merely a temporary disruption. The claim that users' identities are 'known' is unverified and may be a bluff to deter future use. The technical details about protocols (e.g., VLESS/Reality) are interesting but may already be outdated as other services adopt similar or more advanced obfuscation. The actual number of ransomware groups using the service (25) is a claim from law enforcement and may include groups that used it only once.

What to Watch

  • Monitor underground forums for reactions and discussions about alternative criminal VPN services.
  • Track whether any of the identified 506 users are linked to new ransomware attacks or infrastructure.
  • Observe if law enforcement publishes technical indicators (IOCs) like server IPs, TLS certificates, or configuration files from the seized servers.
  • Watch for the emergence of new VPN services that explicitly claim to be 'First VPN replacement' or that adopt more resilient architectures (e.g., decentralized VPNs).
  • Analyze the impact on ransomware attack volumes in the weeks following the takedown.
深度笔记

要点

  • First VPN是一个犯罪VPN服务，在由法国和荷兰领导的“藏红花行动”中被捣毁。
  • 自2014年以来活跃，在27个国家提供32个出口节点，并在俄语网络犯罪论坛上推广。
  • 被至少25个勒索软件团伙（例如Avaddon）用于网络侦察和入侵。
  • 支持的协议：OpenConnect、WireGuard、Outline、VLESS TCP Reality、OpenVPN ECC、L2TP/IPSec、PPtP。
  • 接受匿名支付（比特币、Perfect Money、Webmoney），并提供从每天2美元到每年483美元的订阅服务。
  • 当局查获了33台服务器，在乌克兰讯问了管理员，并通知用户其身份已被知晓。
  • Bitdefender分享了506名用户的数据，指出此类行动提高了网络犯罪活动的成本。

背景与对比

这是已知的首次针对专门面向勒索软件团伙并为其所用的VPN服务进行的取缔行动，与之前针对bulletproof托管服务商或单个勒索软件基础设施的行动形成对比。此次行动的规模（18个国家，33台服务器）以及明确针对“犯罪VPN即服务”的目标，代表了在破坏勒索软件供应链方面的一个新前沿。之前的努力主要集中在取缔勒索软件即服务（RaaS）平台或加密货币交易所；而这次行动针对的是支撑整个生态系统的匿名化层。

重要性

此次行动展示了执法部门在摧毁支撑勒索软件攻击的基础设施方面的强大行动能力。对于研究人员而言，它验证了一个假设：破坏匿名化服务可以对网络犯罪生态系统产生连锁效应，增加运营成本并降低对此类服务的信任。技术细节（协议、支付方式、基础设施位置）为开发基于网络的检测和归因技术提供了宝贵的情报。它也凸显了国际合作在打击网络犯罪基础设施方面的重要性。

批判性视角

虽然这是一次重大胜利，但文章指出“新的匿名化服务将会出现”。长期影响取决于这是否会产生持续的威慑效果，还是仅仅是一次暂时的干扰。关于用户身份“已知”的说法未经证实，可能是一种旨在阻止未来使用的虚张声势。关于协议（例如VLESS/Reality）的技术细节很有趣，但随着其他服务采用类似或更先进的混淆技术，这些细节可能已经过时。使用该服务的勒索软件团伙的实际数量（25个）是执法部门的说法，可能包括仅使用过一次的团伙。

后续关注

  • 监控地下论坛对替代性犯罪VPN服务的反应和讨论。
  • 追踪被识别的506名用户中是否有人与新的勒索软件攻击或基础设施有关联。
  • 观察执法部门是否会公布从被查获服务器中提取的技术指标（IOC），如服务器IP、TLS证书或配置文件。
  • 留意那些明确声称是“First VPN替代品”或采用更具弹性架构（例如去中心化VPN）的新VPN服务的出现。
  • 分析此次行动后数周内勒索软件攻击数量的影响。
