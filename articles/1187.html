Uncovering repurposed medicines to fight liver fibrosis

Uncovering repurposed medicines to fight liver fibrosis

content-management digital-publishing rlhf AI & Machine Learning Others
Summary

Liver fibrosis, a scarring process leading to cirrhosis, causes over 1.4 million deaths annually. Stanford researcher Gary Peltz used Google DeepMind's Co-Scientist AI to identify repurposed drugs for treatment. Co-Scientist proposed three candidates, while Peltz selected two from literature. Testing on live human liver cells showed Peltz's picks had no benefit, but two of Co-Scientist's candidates blocked fibrosis and promoted liver cell regeneration. The standout, cancer drug vorinostat, blocked 91% of a damage response driving scarring. Co-Scientist identified drugs reshaping gene activity, offering a new anti-fibrotic approach.

摘要

肝纤维化是一种导致肝硬化的瘢痕形成过程，每年造成超过140万人死亡。斯坦福大学研究员Gary Peltz利用Google DeepMind的Co-Scientist AI寻找可重用的治疗药物。Co-Scientist提出了三个候选药物，而Peltz从文献中选出两个。在活体人类肝细胞测试中，Peltz的选药无效，但Co-Scientist的两个候选药物阻断了纤维化并促进肝细胞再生。其中抗癌药vorinostat阻断了91%的驱动瘢痕形成的损伤反应。Co-Scientist识别出重塑基因活性的药物，为抗纤维化治疗提供了新方向。
