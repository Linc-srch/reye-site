An OpenAI model has disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry

An OpenAI model has disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry

2026-05-21 · openai_rss

Summary

An OpenAI model has autonomously disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry, the Erdős unit distance problem, which asks for the maximum number of unit-distance pairs among n points in the plane. For nearly 80 years, the prevailing belief was that square-grid constructions were essentially optimal. The AI produced an infinite family of configurations achieving a polynomial improvement, with a fixed exponent δ > 0. The proof, verified by external mathematicians, uses sophisticated ideas from algebraic number theory. This marks the first time an AI has autonomously solved a prominent open problem central to a subfield of mathematics, demonstrating advanced reasoning capabilities.

摘要

一个OpenAI模型自主推翻了几何离散数学中的一个核心猜想，即Erdős单位距离问题。该问题询问平面上n个点之间单位距离对的最大数量。近80年来，主流观点认为方形网格构造基本是最优的。该AI生成了一个无限族配置，实现了多项式改进，具有固定指数δ > 0。该证明经外部数学家验证，运用了代数数论中的复杂思想。这标志着AI首次自主解决了一个数学子领域中的著名开放问题，展示了其高级推理能力。
