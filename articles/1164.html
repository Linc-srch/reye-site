When Critics Disagree: Adaptive Reward Poisoning Attacks in RIS-Aided Wireless Control System

当评论家意见相左时：RIS辅助无线控制系统中的自适应奖励投毒攻击

Deemah H. Tashman, Soumaya Cherkaoui · 2026-05-20 · arxiv

Summary

This paper introduces a novel disagreement-guided reward poisoning attack that exploits SAC's twin-critic uncertainty to maximally degrade performance in RIS-assisted cognitive radio networks, showing that state-adaptive attacks are far more damaging than fixed-timing baselines.

摘要

本文提出了一种新颖的、基于评论家分歧引导的自适应奖励投毒攻击（DGRP），专门针对RIS辅助认知无线电网络中的SAC算法。实验表明，这种状态自适应攻击在降低用户数据速率和RIS效率方面，显著优于传统的周期性或探索触发式攻击，揭示了深度强化学习无线系统面临的新安全威胁。

2) CRGP

C — Context

Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) is increasingly used for real-time control in wireless systems like Cognitive Radio Networks (CRNs) and Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS). However, DRL agents are vulnerable to reward poisoning attacks, where adversaries manipulate the observed reward signal to mislead learning. Existing reward poisoning attacks rely on fixed timing or exploration heuristics and do not adapt to the agent's learning state or uncertainty.

R — Related work

  • White-box targeted reward poisoning with bounded perturbations at attacker-selected timesteps (Sun et al.).
  • Black-box targeted reward poisoning with per-step and total budgets, intervening when action deviates from target policy (Rakhsha et al.).
  • White-box exploration-guided reward poisoning that alters reward at high-exploration timesteps (Kumar et al.).
  • Uniform random and policy-guided reward poisoning with bounded offsets under strict budgets (Zhang et al.).

G — Gap

Prior reward poisoning attacks on DRL are not state-adaptive—they do not read the learner's uncertainty or focus perturbations on high-leverage, high-uncertainty states. Furthermore, they have not been evaluated in realistic wireless communication environments, such as RIS-assisted CRNs, leaving a gap in understanding the impact of adaptive attacks on practical DRL-based wireless control systems.

P — Proposal

The paper proposes Disagreement-Guided Reward Poisoning (DGRP), an adaptive attack on a Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) agent in an RIS-assisted CRN. DGRP corrupts rewards precisely when the SAC dual critics exhibit large disagreement (indicating high-uncertainty states), thereby distorting value estimation and steering the policy toward suboptimal actions. The attack is evaluated against periodic-timing and exploration-triggered baselines in a realistic wireless environment.

4) Experiments

Main results

DGRP reduces the average SU data rate by up to 38% compared to the no-attack baseline, while periodic-timing and exploration-triggered attacks cause reductions of 22% and 27% respectively. The attack also diminishes RIS efficiency, with the RIS gain dropping from 1.8x to 1.2x under DGRP.

Ablation

Increasing the poisoning budget from 0.1 to 0.5 reduces the SU rate by an additional 15%, and lowering the disagreement threshold from 0.5 to 0.2 increases attack effectiveness by 12% in terms of rate degradation.

Limitations

["The attack assumes white-box access to the SAC agent's critic networks and reward signal, which may not hold in all real-world deployments.", 'The evaluation is limited to a specific CRN scenario with a single RIS and static channel models; generalizability to other wireless settings is not verified.', 'The paper does not explore defenses or countermeasures against disagreement-guided attacks.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Demonstrates that state-adaptive attacks exploiting model uncertainty are significantly more damaging than heuristic-based attacks, a key insight for designing robust DRL systems.
  2. Connects adversarial machine learning to wireless system security, highlighting a practical vulnerability in RIS-aided networks that are central to 6G.
  3. Provides a concrete attack methodology that can be used as a benchmark for evaluating robustness of DRL-based control in communication systems.

主要结论 - 提出了一种状态自适应的奖励投毒攻击（DGRP），利用SAC双评论家的分歧来识别高不确定性状态并实施攻击。 - DGRP通过在高不确定性状态中篡改奖励，扭曲价值估计，从而引导策略选择次优动作。 - 在降低SU数据速率和RIS效率方面，DGRP持续优于周期性触发和探索触发基线攻击。 - 攻击效果依赖于投毒预算和分歧阈值；预算越高、阈值越低，造成的破坏越大。 - 强调了在基于DRL的无线系统中，需要进行考虑模型不确定性的鲁棒性评估。

1) 阅读理由

本文提出了一种利用SAC双评论家分歧进行状态自适应奖励投毒的攻击方法，并证明其比固定时序基线攻击更具破坏性。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：深度强化学习（DRL）越来越多地用于认知无线电网络（CRN）和可重构智能表面（RIS）等无线系统的实时控制。然而，DRL智能体容易受到奖励投毒攻击，即对手操纵观察到的奖励信号来误导学习。现有的奖励投毒攻击依赖于固定时序或探索启发式方法，不能适应智能体的学习状态或不确定性。

相关工作：现有工作包括白盒定向奖励投毒（Sun等人）、黑盒定向奖励投毒（Rakhsha等人）、白盒探索引导奖励投毒（Kumar等人）以及均匀随机和策略引导奖励投毒（Zhang等人）。

差距：先前的DRL奖励投毒攻击不是状态自适应的——它们不读取学习者的不确定性，也不将扰动集中在高杠杆、高不确定性的状态上。此外，它们未在现实的无线通信环境（如RIS辅助CRN）中进行评估，这留下了理解自适应攻击对实际DRL无线控制系统影响的空白。

提案：本文提出了分歧引导奖励投毒（DGRP），一种针对RIS辅助CRN中SAC智能体的自适应攻击。DGRP在SAC双评论家表现出较大分歧（指示高不确定性状态）时精确地破坏奖励，从而扭曲价值估计并将策略引向次优动作。该攻击在现实无线环境中与周期性触发和探索触发基线进行了比较评估。

3) 实验

DGRP将平均SU数据速率降低了高达38%，而无攻击基线为0%；周期性触发和探索触发攻击分别导致22%和27%的降低。攻击还降低了RIS效率，RIS增益从1.8倍下降到1.2倍（DGRP下）。

消融实验

将投毒预算从0.1增加到0.5，SU速率额外降低15%；将分歧阈值从0.5降低到0.2，攻击有效性（以速率退化衡量）提高12%。

局限性

['该攻击假设对SAC智能体的评论家网络和奖励信号具有白盒访问权限，这在某些实际部署中可能不成立。', '评估仅限于具有单个RIS和静态信道模型的特定CRN场景；未验证对其他无线设置的泛化能力。', '论文未探讨针对分歧引导攻击的防御或对策。']

4) 重要性

  1. 证明了利用模型不确定性的状态自适应攻击比基于启发式的攻击更具破坏性，这是设计鲁棒DRL系统的关键见解。
  2. 将对抗性机器学习与无线系统安全联系起来，突显了在6G核心的RIS辅助网络中存在的实际漏洞。
  3. 提供了一种具体的攻击方法，可作为评估通信系统中基于DRL控制鲁棒性的基准。
