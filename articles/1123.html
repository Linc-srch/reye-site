Measuring progress toward AGI: A cognitive framework

Measuring progress toward AGI: A cognitive framework

Summary

Google DeepMind introduces a cognitive science-based framework to measure progress toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). The framework defines ten key cognitive abilities—such as perception, learning, reasoning, and social cognition—and proposes a three-stage evaluation protocol comparing AI performance to human baselines. To operationalize this, DeepMind launches a Kaggle hackathon with a $200,000 prize pool, inviting researchers to design evaluations for five under-tested abilities: learning, metacognition, attention, executive functions, and social cognition. The goal is to create empirical tools for tracking AGI development.

摘要

Google DeepMind 提出基于认知科学的框架来衡量通用人工智能（AGI）的进展。该框架定义了十种关键认知能力，如感知、学习、推理和社交认知，并设计了将AI性能与人类基线对比的三阶段评估协议。为落实这一框架，DeepMind 在 Kaggle 上发起奖金池为20万美元的黑客松，邀请研究者针对学习、元认知、注意力、执行功能和社交认知这五种评估缺口最大的能力设计评测任务。
