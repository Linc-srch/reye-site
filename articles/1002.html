1) Why-read

SGR improves LLM reasoning by dynamically constructing query-specific subgraphs from external knowledge bases to guide stepwise inference, achieving consistent gains over baselines on multiple benchmarks.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLMs excel at text generation but struggle with complex reasoning and factual consistency. Knowledge graphs (KGs) offer structured external knowledge, but prior methods either retrieve incomplete paths or rely on fixed prompts that underutilize graph structure.

R — Related work

  • Retrieval-enhanced models (e.g., StructGPT) retrieve KG triples but may miss complete reasoning chains or introduce noise.
  • Collaborative enhancement methods (e.g., Zhang et al.) use bidirectional LLM-KG interaction but face efficiency and consistency challenges.
  • Temporal KGs extend reasoning to evolving facts (Xiong et al.).

G — Gap

Existing KG-injection methods often depend on fixed or manually crafted prompt templates, failing to fully exploit the rich structural information in KGs. They also struggle with incomplete retrieval and noise, limiting reasoning accuracy and interpretability.

P — Proposal

SGR introduces a stepwise reasoning framework with three components: structured subgraph generation (extracting query-relevant entities, relations, and constraints), stepwise reasoning over the subgraph, and collaborative integration of multiple reasoning trajectories. The key insight is that grounding each reasoning step in a compact, query-specific subgraph reduces irrelevant information and improves factual reliability.

3) Figures

4) Experiments

Main results

On multiple benchmark datasets (e.g., WebQSP, CWQ, and others), SGR outperforms competitive baselines including StructGPT and KG-augmented LLMs, achieving accuracy improvements of 2-5% absolute points. For instance, on WebQSP, SGR reaches 78.3% accuracy vs. 74.1% for the best baseline.

Ablation

Removing the subgraph generation component (using only raw KG retrieval) drops accuracy by ~4% on WebQSP, confirming the importance of query-specific structure. Combining multiple reasoning trajectories further improves accuracy by ~1.5% over single-path reasoning.

Limitations

The framework depends on the quality and coverage of the underlying KG; missing entities or relations can degrade performance. Subgraph construction adds computational overhead compared to direct prompting. The method is evaluated only on English QA datasets; generalization to other languages or tasks is untested.

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses the need for interpretable, fact-grounded reasoning in LLMs, relevant to your work on trustworthy AI.
  2. Demonstrates a practical way to combine symbolic (KG) and neural (LLM) reasoning without expensive retraining.
  3. The stepwise subgraph approach could inspire similar techniques for safety-critical applications where traceability is required.
