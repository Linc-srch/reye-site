2) CRGP

C — Context

Reinforcement learning fine-tuning of vision-language-action (VLA) policies improves generalization but is computationally expensive. GRPO has become the dominant critic-free optimization method, but prior work focused on speeding up rollout collection via faster simulators, leaving gradient computation unoptimized.

R — Related work

  • GRPO-based VLA RL fine-tuning (Li et al. 2025a, Lu et al. 2025, Chen et al. 2025b, Ye et al. 2025)
  • World models and faster simulators to reduce rollout cost (Zhu et al. 2025, Liu et al. 2026, Zang et al. 2026)
  • Prompt-level filtering of zero-variance rollout groups (Yu et al. 2025, Zheng et al. 2025, Hu et al. 2025)
  • Token-level gradient selection using entropy or probability shifts (Wang et al. 2025, Khandoga et al. 2026, Hu et al. 2026)
  • Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, Hu et al. 2021) and activation memory reduction (checkpointing, Chen et al. 2016; Korthikanti et al. 2023)

G — Gap

Existing efficiency methods for GRPO either operate at prompt level (filtering whole rollouts) or token level (using indirect proxies like entropy), but do not exploit the fact that within a trajectory, only phases where successful and failed rollouts diverge produce useful learning signal. Gradient computation is wasted on phases the policy already handles after pre-training and supervised fine-tuning.

P — Proposal

PCM is a drop-in modification to GRPO that allocates gradient computation to a fixed budget of probabilistically selected chunks per trajectory. It scores semantic phases using success-failure action variance (C_c), a rollout-derived proxy for per-phase gradient variance, and samples chunks with online-updated phase-level keep probabilities. The method requires no reward model or learned critic.

4) Experiments

Main results

On three LIBERO benchmarks (LIBERO-Object, LIBERO-Goal, LIBERO-Spatial), PCM matches the final success rate of standard GRPO while achieving 2.38x wall-clock speedup, 4.8x faster gradient updates, and 60% lower peak activation memory, backpropagating through fewer than 20% of trajectory chunks.

Ablation

The key ablation is that success-failure action variance (C_c) concentrates on decision-critical phases and serves as a sufficient proxy for per-phase gradient variance (V_c), enabling effective allocation without a reward model or critic.

Limitations

['PCM relies on a deterministic phase labeling rule, which may not generalize to tasks without clear semantic phases.', 'The method assumes binary success/failure rewards; extension to continuous rewards is not explored.', 'Speedup is demonstrated only on LIBERO benchmarks; performance on other VLA domains or real-world robotics is untested.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Shifts focus from rollout speed to gradient compute efficiency, which is the dominant cost in GRPO-based VLA RL (78% of wall-clock time).
  2. Provides a principled, outcome-grounded allocation signal (success-failure action variance) that avoids indirect proxies like entropy.
  3. Demonstrates that significant speedups (2.38x) and memory savings (60%) are possible without sacrificing task success, making VLA RL post-training more practical.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Apply PCM to other VLA RL benchmarks (e.g., CALVIN, MetaWorld) to verify generality.
  • [ ] Extend PCM to continuous reward settings by defining a continuous divergence measure for advantage variance.
  • [ ] Combine PCM with parameter-efficient fine-tuning (e.g., LoRA) for further compute savings.
  • [ ] Investigate adaptive chunk budget scheduling based on training progress or phase-level keep probability dynamics.
