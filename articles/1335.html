Position: AI Safety Requires Effective Controllability

立场论文：AI安全需要有效的可控性

content-management rlhf zero-trust

Yige Li, Yunhao Feng, Jun Sun

启示 · Relevance

This paper's framing of controllability as a distinct property from alignment directly supports your core objective of building automated evaluation frameworks, suggesting you should integrate controllability metrics (e.g., ability to halt or override an agent mid-task) into your temporal safety benchmarks like SafeManip and your runtime defense frameworks like ClawGuard.

该论文将可控性定义为与对齐不同的独立属性，直接支持您构建自动化评估框架的核心目标，建议您将可控性指标（例如在任务中暂停或覆盖智能体的能力）整合到您的时序安全基准（如 SafeManip）和运行时防御框架（如 ClawGuard）中。

Summary

This paper argues that AI safety requires controllability as a distinct property from alignment, and provides a benchmark and framework to evaluate and design for it.

摘要

本文论证了AI安全需要将可控性作为与对齐性并列的独立属性，并提供了评估和设计可控性的基准与框架。通过ControlBench基准测试，作者发现当前的对齐和护栏机制虽然降低了风险，但无法在运行时提供持久、权威且可执行的控制。该研究为部署自主智能体提供了关键的安全设计指导。

Deep Note

Deep Note — controllability

Key Takeaways - AI safety requires controllability as a first-class objective beyond alignment. - Controllability means a system is interruptible, overridable, redirectable, and constrainable at runtime. - Current alignment and guardrails reduce risk but fail to provide persistent, enforceable runtime control. - ControlBench evaluates controllability failures in 900 high-risk agentic tasks. - Proposed CAS architecture introduces explicit control planes, runtime intervention, and auditable interfaces.

1) Why-read

This paper argues that alignment alone is insufficient for safe AI agents and proposes controllability as a necessary complementary objective, backed by a new benchmark and experiments showing the limits of current safeguards.

2) CRGP

C — Context

AI safety research has focused on alignment—training models to follow human preferences and safety policies. While this has improved model behavior, it does not guarantee that a deployed agent can be stopped or overridden in open-ended, tool-using environments. The paper positions controllability as a missing first-class objective.

R — Related work

  • Alignment techniques (RLHF, constitutional AI) that improve model behavior but do not enforce runtime control.
  • Guardrail systems (e.g., Llama Guard, NeMo) that filter inputs/outputs but can be bypassed.
  • Agent safety research on interruptibility and corrigibility (e.g., Orseau & Armstrong, Leike et al.).
  • Runtime monitoring and sandboxing approaches for AI agents.

G — Gap

Existing safety mechanisms provide partial control along different axes but lack a unified framework for persistent, authoritative, and enforceable runtime control. Alignment alone cannot guarantee that an agent will yield to explicit authority under conflicting instructions, long-horizon execution, or adversarial inputs.

P — Proposal

The paper defines controllability as the ability to reliably interrupt, override, redirect, and constrain an AI system at runtime. It introduces ControlBench, a benchmark of 900 high-risk agentic tasks, and proposes the Control Architecture for Safety (CAS), which features explicit control planes, runtime intervention pathways, persistent control states, and auditable decision interfaces.

---

4) Experiments

Main results

OpenClaw-based agents were evaluated on ControlBench across seven risk categories. The baseline agent achieved high Attack Success Rate (ASR). SafeSkills and AutoSkills (skill-level control variants) yielded only marginal ASR reductions, with several categories remaining in the high-ASR region, showing that current safeguards are insufficient.

Ablation

Comparing SafeSkills and AutoSkills against the baseline showed that skill-level safeguards reduce some unsafe behaviors but fail to provide reliable controllability. The marginal improvement indicates that external guardrails alone cannot enforce persistent runtime control.

Limitations

['ControlBench is synthetic and may not capture all real-world agentic risks.', 'The proposed CAS architecture is conceptual and has not been fully implemented or evaluated.', 'Experiments are limited to OpenClaw-based agents; results may not generalize to other agent frameworks.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses a critical gap in AI safety: the need for runtime control beyond alignment.
  2. Provides a concrete benchmark (ControlBench) for evaluating controllability failures.
  3. Proposes a design framework (CAS) that could guide future safe agent architectures.
  4. Highlights that current guardrails are insufficient for high-risk tool-using agents.

深度笔记

立场论文：AI安全需要有效的可控性

主要结论 - 仅靠对齐不足以实现AI安全；可控性必须成为首要目标。 - 可控性意味着系统在运行时能够被可靠地中断、覆盖、重定向和约束。 - 当前的对齐和护栏机制降低了风险，但无法提供持久、权威的运行时控制。 - ControlBench评估了高风险智能体场景中的可控性失效。 - 提出了一个以控制为中心的框架，包含显式控制平面、干预路径和可审计性。

1) 阅读理由

本文的核心主张是AI安全需要将可控性作为独立于对齐的属性，关键观察是当前机制无法保证运行时对智能体的可靠控制。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：AI安全领域长期由RLHF和宪法训练等对齐方法主导，这些方法改善了平均行为。然而，当模型成为能够规划、使用工具并进行长程交互的智能体时，在运行时停止或覆盖它们的能力变得至关重要，但对齐无法保证这一点。

相关工作：基于训练的对齐方法（RLHF、DPO、宪法AI）塑造行为但缺乏运行时权威；可控生成和解码方法在长程交互中脆弱；激活和表示工程提供细粒度干预但不强制执行系统级控制；外部护栏和内容审核（如Llama Guard）减少表面危害但可被绕过；关于可中断性、可修正性和可关闭性的先前工作（如Soares等人，2015）激发了运行时控制的需求。

差距：现有安全机制专注于使模型在期望上安全，但无法保证部署的智能体在冲突指令、长程执行或对抗输入下能被停止、覆盖或约束。缺乏系统评估可控性作为独立属性的基准。

提案：本文引入了ControlBench，一个评估智能体场景中可控性失效的基准，并提出了一个以控制为中心的架构框架（CAS），包含五个要求：权威性、可中断性、运行时可执行性、持久性和可审计性。关键见解是可控性必须被设计为显式的运行时属性，而不仅仅是训练目标。

3) 实验

基于OpenClaw智能体的实验表明，当前的对齐和护栏机制在涉及冲突指令、停止信号和危险工具使用的场景中降低了风险，但无法提供持久、权威且可执行的运行时控制。

消融实验

本文未包含传统的消融研究，但基准测试结果表明，移除护栏会增加失败率，而添加护栏并不能在所有条件下保证可控性。

局限性

['基准测试仅限于基于OpenClaw的智能体，可能无法推广到所有智能体架构。', '提出的框架是概念性的，缺乏完整的实现或实证验证。', '本文未解决如何设计既可执行又保留效用的控制机制。']

4) 重要性

  1. 揭示了当前AI安全研究中与部署自主智能体直接相关的关键空白。
  2. 提供了具体的基准（ControlBench），可用于评估自身系统的可控性。
  3. 将焦点从训练时对齐转向运行时控制，这对安全关键应用至关重要。
  4. 提供了结构化框架（CAS），可指导未来可控AI系统的设计。
