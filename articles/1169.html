What Are LLMs Doing to Scientific Communication? Measuring Changes in Writing Practices and Reading Experience

大型语言模型对科学交流的影响：写作实践与阅读体验变化的测量

Filip Miletić, Neele Falk

Summary

This paper provides the first large-scale, mixed-methods evidence that LLM-assisted writing is measurably changing scientific communication style and that these changes affect reader perceptions in complex, subjective ways.

摘要

本文通过分析37,000篇ACL论文（2020-2024年）和3,000对人类-LLM写作对，首次大规模混合方法证明了LLM辅助写作正在显著改变科学交流风格，并影响读者感知。研究发现LLM改进的文本更易理解、更令人兴奋，但也引发了对AI的负面态度，揭示了信任悖论。

Deep Note

Deep Note — llm-scientific-communication

Key Takeaways - Post-ChatGPT NLP papers show increased lexical complexity and lower lexical diversity, attributable to LLM use. - LLM-improved texts are rated by domain experts as more understandable and exciting, but also provoke negative attitudes toward AI. - The study combines a naturalistic corpus (37k ACL papers, 2020-2024) with a synthetic dataset (3k human-LLM pairs) to isolate LLM effects. - Semantic shifts in word usage (e.g., specialization vs. generalization) are detected via diachronic lexical analyses. - Subjective reading experience is strongly influenced by awareness of LLM involvement, highlighting a trust paradox.

1) Why-read

This paper provides the first large-scale, mixed-methods evidence that LLM-assisted writing is measurably changing scientific communication style and that these changes affect reader perceptions in complex, subjective ways.

2) CRGP

C — Context

The rapid adoption of LLMs for writing assistance has raised concerns about homogenization and loss of authorial voice in scientific texts. Prior work focused on detecting fully AI-generated text or comparing human vs. machine writing, but rarely examined the collaborative human-LLM writing process or linked stylistic shifts to reader experience.

R — Related work

  • Detection of AI-generated content (e.g., watermarking, classifier-based methods) struggles with human-AI coauthored text.
  • Stylistic comparisons of human vs. AI text show lower lexical diversity, more positive sentiment, and overuse of specific verbs (e.g., 'delve', 'crucial').
  • Prior studies on scientific abstracts (Ma et al., 2023) or news (Muñoz-Ortiz et al., 2024) rarely analyze full papers or subjective perception.
  • Lin and Zhu (2025) find researchers use LLMs to improve clarity, leading to more homogeneous writing since ChatGPT.

G — Gap

Existing work oversimplifies LLM use by comparing fully human vs. fully AI-generated text, ignoring the common practice of LLM-assisted editing. Moreover, the link between objective stylistic changes and subjective reading experience (e.g., trust, excitement) remains unexplored.

P — Proposal

The authors create two datasets: (1) a naturalistic corpus of 37k ACL papers (2020-2024) split into pre- and post-ChatGPT periods, and (2) a synthetic dataset of 3,000 human-written passages paired with LLM-improved versions. They combine diachronic lexical analysis, stylistic feature modeling, and a pilot annotation study with 20 domain experts to connect writing changes to reading experience.

---

4) Experiments

Main results

Diachronic lexical analysis reveals significant shifts in word frequency and usage contexts post-ChatGPT. Stylistic modeling shows LLM-modified texts have lower lexical diversity, more complex/longer words, and more frequent certain syntactic constructions. In the annotation study, 20 experts rated LLM-improved texts as more understandable and exciting, but expressed negative qualitative attitudes toward LLMs.

Ablation

Not explicitly reported as an ablation; the study relies on comparing naturalistic vs. synthetic data to attribute changes to LLM use.

Limitations

['The pilot annotation study is small (20 experts, 200 pairs) and may not generalize.', 'The naturalistic corpus cannot fully control for confounding factors (e.g., evolving community norms).', 'The synthetic dataset uses a single LLM (likely GPT-4) and may not reflect diverse LLM usage patterns.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly relevant to understanding how LLMs reshape academic writing norms in NLP and beyond.
  2. Provides a methodological template for studying human-AI collaborative writing at scale.
  3. Highlights the tension between improved clarity/readability and diminished trust/authenticity, critical for AI safety and digital identity.
  4. Offers concrete evidence for the 'homogenization' hypothesis of AI-assisted writing.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Extend the annotation study to larger, more diverse expert panels and non-expert readers.
  • [ ] Investigate whether stylistic changes affect citation impact or reproducibility of findings.
  • [ ] Develop interventions (e.g., disclosure guidelines) to mitigate trust erosion while preserving clarity gains.
  • [ ] Apply similar methodology to other scientific domains (e.g., biomedicine, physics).

深度笔记

大型语言模型对科学交流的影响：写作实践与阅读体验变化的测量

主要结论 - ChatGPT后的NLP论文词汇复杂度增加、词汇多样性降低，归因于LLM使用。 - 领域专家认为LLM改进的文本更易理解和令人兴奋，但对AI持负面态度。 - 研究结合自然语料库（37k ACL论文）和合成数据集（3k人类-LLM对）以隔离LLM效应。 - 通过历时词汇分析检测到词汇使用的语义变化（如专业化vs.泛化）。 - 主观阅读体验受LLM参与意识强烈影响，凸显信任悖论。

1) 阅读理由

本文首次提供大规模混合方法证据，表明LLM辅助写作正在可测量地改变科学交流风格，并且这些改变以复杂、主观的方式影响读者感知。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：LLM在写作辅助中的快速应用引发了对科学文本同质化和作者声音丧失的担忧。先前工作侧重于检测完全AI生成的文本或比较人类与机器写作，但很少考察人类-LLM协作写作过程或将风格变化与读者体验联系起来。

相关工作：AI生成内容检测（如水印、分类器方法）难以处理人-AI合著文本；人类与AI文本的风格比较显示词汇多样性降低、情感更积极、特定动词过度使用；先前研究（Ma等人2023；Muñoz-Ortiz等人2024）很少分析完整论文或主观感知；Lin和Zhu（2025）发现研究者使用LLM提高清晰度，导致ChatGPT后写作更同质化。

差距：现有工作通过比较完全人类与完全AI生成文本简化了LLM使用，忽略了LLM辅助编辑的常见实践。此外，客观风格变化与主观阅读体验（如信任、兴奋）之间的联系尚未探索。

提议：作者创建两个数据集：（1）37k篇ACL论文（2020-2024年）的自然语料库，分为ChatGPT前后时期；（2）3,000对人类撰写段落与LLM改进版本的合成数据集。他们结合历时词汇分析、风格特征建模和20位领域专家的试点标注研究，将写作变化与阅读体验联系起来。

3) 实验

历时词汇分析显示ChatGPT后词频和使用语境发生显著变化。风格建模显示LLM修改的文本词汇多样性降低、单词更复杂/更长、某些句法结构更频繁。在标注研究中，20位专家认为LLM改进的文本更易理解和令人兴奋，但对LLM表达了负面定性态度。

消融实验

未明确报告为消融实验；研究依赖比较自然语料与合成数据来归因LLM效应。

局限性

试点标注研究规模小（20位专家，200对），可能无法推广；自然语料库无法完全控制混杂因素（如不断演变的社区规范）；合成数据集使用单一LLM（可能为GPT-4），可能无法反映多样化的LLM使用模式。

4) 重要性

  1. 直接相关于理解LLM如何重塑NLP及更广泛领域的学术写作规范。
  2. 为大规模研究人类-AI协作写作提供了方法论模板。
  3. 凸显了清晰度/可读性提升与信任/真实性下降之间的张力，对AI安全和数字身份至关重要。
  4. 为AI辅助写作的“同质化”假说提供了具体证据。

5) 下一步

  • [ ] 将标注研究扩展到更大、更多样化的专家小组和非专家读者。
  • [ ] 调查风格变化是否影响引用影响力或研究结果的可重复性。
  • [ ] 制定干预措施（如披露指南），以在保持清晰度收益的同时减轻信任侵蚀。
  • [ ] 将类似方法应用于其他科学领域（如生物医学、物理学）。
