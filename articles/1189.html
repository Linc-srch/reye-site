Accelerating discovery of liver disease mechanisms

Accelerating discovery of liver disease mechanisms

2026-05-20 · deepmind_blog

Summary

Google DeepMind's Co-Scientist, used by University of Edinburgh researchers, accelerates liver disease research by synthesizing vast biomedical literature to generate novel hypotheses. Focusing on metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH), the system identified the NLRP3 inflammasome as a key molecular bridge linking inflammation and metabolism, a connection previously unrecognized. This hypothesis, experimentally verified, explains why the drug resmetirom helps only a narrow patient subset and suggests targeted dual-therapies. Co-Scientist narrows the search among overwhelming drug combinations, acting as a 'jetpack' to shorten iteration cycles for breakthroughs.

摘要

Google DeepMind的Co-Scientist被爱丁堡大学研究人员用于加速肝病研究，通过综合海量生物医学文献生成新假设。针对代谢功能障碍相关脂肪性肝炎（MASH），该系统识别出NLRP3炎症小体作为连接炎症与代谢的关键分子桥梁，这一此前未被发现的联系经实验验证，解释了药物resmetirom仅对少数患者有效的原因，并提示了靶向联合疗法。Co-Scientist在大量药物组合中缩小搜索范围，如同科学家的“喷气背包”，缩短了突破性发现的迭代周期。
