Our response to the TanStack npm supply chain attack

Our response to the TanStack npm supply chain attack

Summary

OpenAI disclosed a security incident involving the TanStack npm library, part of the broader Mini Shai-Hulud supply chain attack. Two employee devices were compromised, leading to limited credential exfiltration from internal source code repositories. No evidence of user data, production system, or intellectual property compromise was found. As a precaution, OpenAI is rotating code-signing certificates, requiring macOS users to update apps by June 12, 2026. The company has implemented additional security controls and is coordinating with platform providers to prevent unauthorized certificate use. This incident highlights the growing threat of supply chain attacks targeting shared software dependencies.

摘要

OpenAI披露了一起涉及TanStack npm库的安全事件，该事件是更广泛的Mini Shai-Hulud供应链攻击的一部分。两台员工设备被入侵，导致内部源代码仓库中的有限凭据被窃取。没有证据表明用户数据、生产系统或知识产权受损。作为预防措施，OpenAI正在轮换代码签名证书，要求macOS用户在2026年6月12日前更新应用。公司已实施额外的安全控制，并与平台提供商协调以防止证书被滥用。此事件凸显了针对共享软件依赖的供应链攻击威胁日益增长。
