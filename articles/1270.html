Cyber Pros Can't Decide If AI Is a Good or a Bad Thing

Cyber Pros Can't Decide If AI Is a Good or a Bad Thing

rlhf supply-chain-attack zero-trust
Summary

This article discusses the ambivalence among cybersecurity professionals regarding the impact of artificial intelligence on their field. While AI offers powerful tools for threat detection and automated response, it also enables more sophisticated cyberattacks, such as AI-generated phishing and deepfakes. The piece highlights survey data showing that many cyber pros are uncertain whether AI ultimately helps or harms security efforts. This split reflects the dual-use nature of AI, where the same technology that strengthens defenses can also be weaponized by adversaries. The article underscores the need for adaptive strategies and ethical guidelines to navigate this evolving landscape.

摘要

本文探讨了网络安全专业人士对AI影响的矛盾态度。一方面，AI增强了威胁检测和自动响应能力；另一方面，它也被用于生成更复杂的钓鱼攻击和deepfake。调查显示，许多从业者不确定AI整体上对安全是有益还是有害。这种分歧反映了AI的双重用途特性，即同一技术既可强化防御也可被对手武器化。文章强调了制定适应性策略和伦理准则以应对这一演变格局的必要性。
