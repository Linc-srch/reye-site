Draft Less, Retrieve More: Hybrid Tree Construction for Speculative Decoding

少草稿，多检索：面向推测解码的混合树构建方法

sparse-attention differentiable-routing hierarchical-attention

Yuhao Shen, Tianyu Liu, Xinyi Hu, Quan Kong, Baolin Zhang, Jun Dai, Jun Zhang, Shuang Ge, Lei Chen, Yue Li, Mingcheng Wan, Cong Wang

Summary

Graft introduces a prune-then-graft framework that reuses pruned slots for retrieval, overcoming the inherent MAT loss of dynamic pruning and achieving state-of-the-art speedups in speculative decoding.

摘要

本文提出了一种名为Graft的推测解码框架，通过“剪枝-嫁接”机制，将动态剪枝释放的计算预算用于检索，从而打破了延迟与平均接受长度之间的帕累托权衡。该方法无需训练、无损，并在短上下文、长上下文和大规模设置下均实现了新的帕累托前沿，在Qwen3-235B上最高获得5.41倍加速。

Deep Note

Deep Note — graft

Key Takeaways - Graft breaks the latency-MAT tradeoff in speculative decoding by using retrieval to fill slots freed by pruning. - Pruning releases computational budget; retrieval compensates for coverage loss with near-zero overhead. - Graft is training-free, lossless, and establishes a new Pareto frontier across short-context, long-context, and large-scale settings. - On Qwen3-235B, Graft achieves up to 5.41x speedup and improves average speedup over EAGLE-3 by 21.8%. - The method scales naturally to long contexts: longer prompts boost retrieval hit rates while making drafting more expensive.

1) Why-read

Graft introduces a prune-then-graft framework that reuses pruned slots for retrieval, overcoming the inherent MAT loss of dynamic pruning and achieving state-of-the-art speedups in speculative decoding.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Speculative decoding accelerates LLM inference via a draft-then-verify paradigm. Recent methods build large draft trees to maximize acceptance rate, but this incurs severe VRAM and compute overhead. Dynamic-depth pruning reduces this cost but discards valid candidates, creating a strict latency-MAT tradeoff.

R — Related work

  • EAGLE-3: dense tree drafting using target-model features, achieving high MAT but high overhead.
  • Dynamic pruning methods (DDD, SVIP, ECHO): prune low-confidence branches to reduce cost, but MAT is bounded below dense trees.
  • Retrieval-based SD (SAM-Decoding, PLD, REST, Token Recycle): use external sources but often as standalone drafters or with CPU-side overhead.
  • Block drafting (DFlash): parallel block-level speculation, extended by Graft in preliminary experiments.

G — Gap

Existing dynamic pruning methods strictly produce subtrees of the original dense tree, so confidence misjudgments inevitably reduce the mean accepted length below the dense-tree upper bound. This creates an unsolved Pareto tradeoff between speed and MAT that pruning alone cannot overcome.

P — Proposal

Graft couples pruning and retrieval in a sequential prune-then-graft mechanism: first prune low-confidence branches to release budget, then graft highly predictive retrieved tokens into the vacated slots. Retrieval is rooted at the current token, prepared in parallel with drafting, and uses a GPU-resident adjacency matrix with online updates, keeping overhead near zero and preserving lossless verification.

---

4) Experiments

Main results

On short-context benchmarks, Graft achieves up to 5.41x speedup and improves average speedup over EAGLE-3 by 21.8% on Qwen3-235B. On long-context LLaMA3.1-8B, it reaches 3.22x average speedup; on Qwen3-14B, it outperforms EAGLE3-64K by 16.6%.

Ablation

Ablation confirms that both pruning and retrieval are essential: pruning alone reduces MAT below dense tree, but adding retrieval recovers and even exceeds dense-tree MAT while maintaining lower latency.

Limitations

['Retrieval effectiveness depends on context diversity; repetitive or highly novel sequences may yield lower hit rates.', 'The current retrieval adjacency matrix is limited to local n-gram patterns and may not capture long-range dependencies.', 'Preliminary DFlash extension is exploratory and not yet fully optimized for block drafting paradigms.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses the core latency bottleneck in LLM inference, relevant to any deployment requiring high throughput.
  2. Shows that pruning and retrieval can be complementary rather than competing, a design insight applicable beyond SD.
  3. Training-free and lossless integration makes it practical for production systems without additional fine-tuning.
  4. Scales naturally with context length, aligning with the trend toward long-context models.

1) 阅读理由

Graft提出了一种“先剪枝后嫁接”的框架，重用剪枝槽位进行检索，克服了动态剪枝固有的平均接受长度损失，在推测解码中实现了最先进的加速效果。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

上下文：推测解码通过“草稿-验证”范式加速大语言模型推理。近期方法构建大型草稿树以最大化接受率，但带来了严重的显存和计算开销。动态深度剪枝降低了这一成本，但丢弃了有效候选，造成了严格的延迟-平均接受长度权衡。相关工作：EAGLE-3使用目标模型特征构建密集草稿树，平均接受长度高但开销大；动态剪枝方法（DDD、SVIP、ECHO）剪枝低置信度分支以降低成本，但平均接受长度受限于密集树；基于检索的推测解码（SAM-Decoding、PLD、REST、Token Recycle）使用外部源，但通常作为独立草稿器或存在CPU端开销；块草稿（DFlash）实现并行块级推测，Graft在初步实验中对其进行了扩展。差距：现有动态剪枝方法严格产生原始密集树的子树，因此置信度误判不可避免地使平均接受长度低于密集树上界。这造成了剪枝无法克服的速度与平均接受长度之间的帕累托权衡。提案：Graft在顺序的“剪枝-嫁接”机制中耦合了剪枝和检索：首先剪枝低置信度分支以释放预算，然后将高预测性的检索令牌嫁接到空出的槽位中。检索以当前令牌为根，与草稿生成并行准备，使用GPU驻留的邻接矩阵并在线更新，保持近零开销并实现无损验证。

3) 实验

在短上下文基准测试中，Graft在Qwen3-235B上实现最高5.41倍加速，平均加速比EAGLE-3提升21.8%。在长上下文LLaMA3.1-8B上，平均加速达到3.22倍；在Qwen3-14B上，比EAGLE3-64K提升16.6%。

消融实验

消融实验证实剪枝和检索均不可或缺：仅剪枝会使平均接受长度低于密集树，但加入检索后平均接受长度恢复甚至超过密集树，同时保持更低延迟。

局限性

['检索效果依赖于上下文多样性；重复或高度新颖的序列可能导致较低的命中率。', '当前的检索邻接矩阵局限于局部n-gram模式，可能无法捕捉长距离依赖。', '初步的DFlash扩展尚处于探索阶段，未针对块草稿范式进行充分优化。']

4) 重要性

  1. 直接解决了大语言模型推理中的核心延迟瓶颈，适用于任何需要高吞吐量的部署场景。
  2. 展示了剪枝和检索可以互补而非竞争，这一设计洞察可推广至推测解码之外。
  3. 无需训练且无损集成，使其无需额外微调即可用于生产系统。
  4. 自然随上下文长度扩展，与长上下文模型的发展趋势一致。
