What Parameter Golf taught us about AI-assisted research

What Parameter Golf taught us about AI-assisted research

2026-05-19 · openai_rss

Summary

Parameter Golf was an open machine learning challenge by OpenAI with over 1,000 participants and 2,000 submissions. Participants minimized held-out loss on FineWeb under strict constraints: 16 MB artifact limit and 10-minute training on 8×H100s. The challenge revealed widespread use of AI coding agents, which lowered experimentation costs and accelerated innovation but introduced issues in submission review and attribution. Notable techniques included Muon weight decay, GPTQ-lite quantization, test-time training, and novel tokenizers like CaseOps. The competition also served as a talent discovery platform, highlighting the impact of AI agents on research workflows and community dynamics.
