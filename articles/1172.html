Streamlined Constraint Reasoning via CNN Pattern Recognition on Enumerated Solutions

基于CNN模式识别的枚举解流线约束推理

content-management sparse-attention hierarchical-attention

Patrick Spracklen

Summary

This paper introduces a novel streamliner-synthesis pipeline that uses CNN pattern recognition on enumerated solutions to ground LLM-generated constraints in observed structure, achieving dramatic speedups on hardened CP benchmarks.

摘要

本文提出了一种新颖的流线约束合成流水线，利用CNN对枚举解进行模式识别，将LLM生成的约束建立在观察到的解结构上，在硬化的约束规划基准上实现了显著加速。该方法在Vessel Loading、Social Golfers和Black Hole问题上分别实现了98.8%、98.6%和89.4%的求解时间缩减，最佳单流线约束的几何平均加速比高达932倍、356倍和1103倍。

1) Why-read

This paper introduces a novel streamliner-synthesis pipeline that uses CNN pattern recognition on enumerated solutions to ground LLM-generated constraints in observed structure, achieving dramatic speedups on hardened CP benchmarks.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Constraint programming (CP) practitioners accelerate hard problems by applying sound techniques (symmetry-breaking and implied constraints) first, then resort to streamliner constraints that restrict search to a structural sub-family of solutions but risk incompleteness. Prior automated streamliner synthesis either searches a constraint grammar or prompts an LLM directly on the problem model, lacking grounding in actual solution patterns.

R — Related work

  • Gomes and Sellmann introduced streamlining as a principled incomplete-search technique for CSPs.
  • Wetter, Akgün, and Miguel proposed constraint-lattice search over the Essence modelling language via Conjure.
  • Spracklen et al. extended this with Monte-Carlo Tree Search, optimization support, and portfolio-based selection.
  • StreamLLM prompts an LLM with the MiniZinc model to generate streamliners with runtime feedback, but lacks grounding in observed solution structure.

G — Gap

Existing automated streamliner-synthesis methods either rely on a hand-crafted constraint grammar (limiting discoverable shapes) or prompt an LLM solely on the problem model text, without access to actual solution structure. Moreover, prior evaluations often use unhardened models, conflating recovery of missing standard constraints with genuine streamliner discovery.

P — Proposal

The pipeline enumerates feasible solutions, trains a CNN contrastively against perturbed non-solutions to detect structural patterns, then translates the CNN's discriminative signal into candidate MiniZinc streamliners via LLM-driven synthesis. Two complementary prompting paths are used: filter-property correlation and direct filter-contrast between high- and low-activation solutions.

---

4) Experiments

Main results

Pipeline achieves 98.8% portfolio time reduction on hardened Vessel Loading, 98.6% on hardened Social Golfers, and 89.4% on Black Hole. Best-single streamliners yield geometric-mean speedups of 932x, 356x, and 1103x respectively. On Black Hole, the pipeline matches StreamLLM's realtime result with simple-top-3 and exceeds it with family-budget allocation.

Ablation

Family-budget portfolio allocation (k=3 families, m=3 members) improves over simple top-3 selection on every problem; the largest gain is on Black Hole, where simple-top-3 is dominated by redundant pile-top variants.

Limitations

['Requires enumeration of at least 500 feasible solutions per training instance, which may be expensive for very hard problems.', 'CNN pattern recognition is limited to structural properties that can be captured by the chosen library of ≈30 properties.', 'LLM-driven synthesis may still produce invalid or redundant constraints, requiring validation against cached baselines.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Demonstrates that grounding LLM generation in observed solution structure yields qualitatively distinct and effective streamliners compared to model-text-only approaches.
  2. Highlights the importance of evaluating streamliner synthesis on hardened benchmarks to isolate genuine discovery from recovery of missing standard constraints.
  3. Provides a practical pipeline that combines deep learning pattern recognition with LLM reasoning for automated constraint discovery.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Apply the pipeline to additional CP benchmark families with known hardening to further validate generality.
  • [ ] Explore automated selection of the property library to reduce manual engineering effort.
  • [ ] Investigate integration of the CNN-based pattern recognition directly into the constraint solver loop for online streamliner discovery.
  • [ ] Compare the discovered streamliners with those from grammar-based methods on a common hardened benchmark suite.

1) 阅读理由

本文提出了一种新颖的流线约束合成流水线，利用CNN对枚举解进行模式识别，将LLM生成的约束建立在观察到的解结构上，在硬化的CP基准上实现了显著加速。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

约束规划（CP）从业者通过先应用对称性破缺和隐含约束等可靠技术来加速困难问题，然后使用流线约束将搜索限制在解的结构子族中，但存在不完备风险。先前的自动流线约束合成要么搜索约束语法，要么直接基于问题模型提示LLM，缺乏对实际解模式的利用。相关工作包括Gomes和Sellmann提出的流线约束作为CSP的完备性不完全搜索技术；Wetter、Akgün和Miguel通过Conjure在Essence建模语言上提出的约束格搜索；Spracklen等人将其扩展到蒙特卡洛树搜索、优化支持和基于组合的选择；StreamLLM通过MiniZinc模型提示LLM生成流线约束并利用运行时反馈，但缺乏对解结构的利用。现有方法的空白在于：要么依赖手工制作的约束语法（限制可发现形状），要么仅基于问题模型文本提示LLM，无法访问实际解结构。此外，先前评估常使用未硬化模型，混淆了缺失标准约束的恢复与真正的流线约束发现。本文提出的流水线枚举可行解，通过对比训练CNN检测结构模式，然后将CNN的判别信号通过LLM驱动合成转化为候选MiniZinc流线约束。采用两种互补提示路径：滤波器属性相关性和高/低激活解之间的直接滤波器对比。

3) 实验

流水线在硬化的Vessel Loading上实现了98.8%的求解时间缩减，在硬化的Social Golfers上实现了98.6%，在Black Hole上实现了89.4%。最佳单流线约束的几何平均加速比分别达到932倍、356倍和1103倍。在Black Hole问题上，流水线使用简单top-3选择匹配了StreamLLM的实时结果，使用族预算分配则超越了它。

消融实验

族预算组合分配（k=3个族，m=3个成员）在每个问题上都优于简单的top-3选择；最大增益出现在Black Hole问题上，其中简单top-3被冗余的堆顶变体主导。

局限性

['每个训练实例需要枚举至少500个可行解，对于非常困难的问题可能代价高昂。', 'CNN模式识别仅限于所选约30个属性库能捕获的结构性质。', 'LLM驱动的合成仍可能产生无效或冗余约束，需要针对缓存基线进行验证。']

4) 重要性

  1. 证明了将LLM生成建立在观察到的解结构上，能产生与仅基于模型文本方法截然不同且更有效的流线约束。
  2. 强调了在硬化基准上评估流线约束合成的重要性，以隔离真正的发现与缺失标准约束的恢复。
  3. 提供了一个结合深度学习模式识别与LLM推理的实用流水线，用于自动约束发现。

5) 下一步

  • [ ] 探索属性库的自动选择，以减少手动工程工作。
  • [ ] 研究将CNN模式识别直接集成到约束求解器循环中，实现在线流线约束发现。
