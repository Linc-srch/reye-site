Less Back-and-Forth: A Comparative Study of Structured Prompting

减少来回：结构化提示的对比研究

Saurav Ghosh, Gabriella Polach, Abdou Sow  ·  2026-05-20  ·  arxiv  ·  Original →

Summary

This paper provides empirical evidence that a simple prompt checklist can improve LLM response quality and reduce user effort more effectively than raw prompts or interactive clarifying questions.

摘要

本文通过对比原始提示、清单改进提示和澄清问题提示三种策略，发现清单提示在输出质量和交互效率上均表现最优。研究覆盖四种任务类型和三种大语言模型，为设计高效的人机交互界面提供了实证依据。

1) Why-read

This paper provides empirical evidence that a simple prompt checklist can improve LLM response quality and reduce user effort more effectively than raw prompts or interactive clarifying questions.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLMs are widely used for open-ended tasks, but underspecified prompts often lead to low-quality answers and require multiple follow-up interactions. Prior work has shown that prompt design significantly affects output quality, but there is a lack of controlled comparisons between different structured prompting strategies across tasks and models.

R — Related work

  • Prompt engineering studies show that clear, specific prompts improve LLM response quality (Meskó2023Prompt, etc.).
  • Interactive human-AI interaction research highlights that users often revise prompts after seeing model responses, and clarification systems can reduce effort.
  • Evaluation of LLM output quality now considers factors beyond correctness, such as readability, usability, and user satisfaction.
  • Automated prompt optimization can match or exceed human-crafted prompts, but effectiveness varies by model and task.

G — Gap

While many claim that 'better prompts' lead to better results, there is limited empirical evidence comparing the benefits of simple prompt rewriting versus interactive clarification across different tasks and models. The tradeoff between output quality and interaction effort (tokens, turns) is not well quantified.

P — Proposal

The authors conduct a small-scale comparative study of three prompt conditions (raw, checklist-improved, clarifying-question) across four task types (summarization, planning, explanation, coding) and three LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Grok). They evaluate output quality using a unified rubric (task completion, correctness, compliance, clarity) and measure interaction effort via tokens and turns-to-acceptance.

3) Figures

Figure extraction is performed separately and inlined post-generation.

4) Experiments

Main results

Checklist-improved prompts achieved a mean rubric score of 7.50/8, compared to 5.67 for raw prompts and 6.67 for clarifying-question prompts. Checklist prompts also used fewer average tokens than both raw and clarifying prompts, indicating a better quality-effort tradeoff.

Ablation

The study does not include a formal ablation, but the comparison across three prompt conditions serves as a controlled experiment: checklist prompts consistently outperformed both raw and clarifying-question prompts across tasks and models.

Limitations

['Small-scale study with only 4 tasks and 3 models, limiting generalizability.', 'Human operators evaluated each model separately, introducing potential operator bias.', 'The study does not explore automated prompt optimization or user expertise levels.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Demonstrates that simple, non-interactive prompt structuring (checklist) can be more effective than interactive clarification, which has implications for designing efficient human-AI interfaces.
  2. Provides a practical, low-cost method for improving LLM output quality without requiring user expertise or multiple interaction turns.
  3. Highlights the importance of measuring both quality and effort, aligning with user-centered evaluation in AI systems.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Replicate the study with a larger set of tasks and models to confirm generalizability.
  • [ ] Investigate automated generation of checklist prompts based on task type.
  • [ ] Explore user studies to measure subjective satisfaction and perceived effort alongside objective metrics.
  • [ ] Compare checklist prompting with other structured approaches like chain-of-thought or role-based prompting.

深度笔记

减少来回：结构化提示的对比研究

主要结论 - 清单改进提示的平均评分达到7.50/8，显著高于原始提示的5.67和澄清问题提示的6.67。 - 清单提示在质量-努力权衡上表现最佳，使用的平均token数少于原始提示和澄清问题提示。 - 结构化提示能减少交互努力并提升输出质量，该结论在不同任务类型和LLM上具有一致性。 - 澄清问题提示并未优于清单提示，推翻了交互式澄清能带来更好结果的假设。

1) 阅读理由

本文提供实证证据表明，简单的提示清单比原始提示或交互式澄清问题能更有效地提升LLM响应质量并减少用户努力。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：LLM广泛用于开放式任务，但未充分指定的提示常导致低质量答案并需要多次后续交互。先前研究表明提示设计显著影响输出质量，但缺乏对不同结构化提示策略在任务和模型间的受控比较。

相关工作：提示工程研究显示清晰具体的提示能提升LLM响应质量；人机交互研究强调用户常根据模型响应修改提示，澄清系统可减少努力；LLM输出质量评估已考虑可读性、可用性和用户满意度等因素；自动提示优化可匹配或超越人工提示，但效果因模型和任务而异。

差距：虽然许多研究声称“更好的提示”带来更好的结果，但比较简单提示重写与交互式澄清在不同任务和模型上的收益的实证证据有限，输出质量与交互努力（token数、轮次）之间的权衡也未得到充分量化。

提案：作者开展了一项小规模对比研究，涵盖三种提示条件（原始、清单改进、澄清问题）、四种任务类型（摘要、规划、解释、编码）和三种LLM（ChatGPT、Claude、Grok）。他们使用统一评分标准（任务完成度、正确性、合规性、清晰度）评估输出质量，并通过token数和接受轮次衡量交互努力。

3) 实验

主要结果：清单改进提示的平均评分达到7.50/8，原始提示为5.67，澄清问题提示为6.67。清单提示使用的平均token数也少于原始提示和澄清问题提示，表明其具有更好的质量-努力权衡。

消融实验

该研究未包含正式的消融实验，但三种提示条件的对比本身构成了受控实验：清单提示在所有任务和模型上始终优于原始提示和澄清问题提示。

局限性

['小规模研究，仅包含4种任务和3种模型，限制了泛化性。', '人类操作员分别评估每个模型，可能引入操作员偏差。', '研究未探索自动提示优化或用户专业水平的影响。']

4) 重要性

  1. 表明简单的非交互式提示结构化（清单）可能比交互式澄清更有效，这对设计高效的人机界面具有启示意义。
  2. 提供了一种实用且低成本的方法来提升LLM输出质量，无需用户专业知识或多轮交互。
  3. 强调了同时衡量质量和努力的重要性，与以用户为中心的AI系统评估理念一致。
