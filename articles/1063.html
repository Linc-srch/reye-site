1) Why-read

This paper exposes the 'Cartesian Illusion' in MLLMs and proposes a two-stage, sensory-bounded reasoning pipeline for second-order Theory of Mind that significantly outperforms baselines in occluded multi-agent scenarios.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Multi-Modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) excel at general reasoning but struggle with embodied spatial intelligence, often relying on text-based probability distributions without grounded 3D understanding. Prior work in Embodied Question Answering and audio-visual learning has focused on first-order, single-agent tasks, assuming shared global coordinate systems.

R — Related work

  • Embodied Question Answering (EQA) and spatial reasoning with 3D-LLM, LEO, Video-3D LLM, Ego4D, EPIC-Kitchens, SQA3D
  • Audio-visual learning platforms like SoundSpaces and audio-visual QA benchmarks (e.g., AST)
  • Text-based Theory of Mind (ToM) models (e.g., Mao et al., Sap et al., Ullman)

G — Gap

Existing MLLMs and EQA systems assume omnidirectional, shared spatial awareness (the 'Cartesian Illusion'), failing in multi-agent scenarios requiring second-order ToM. They cannot model an agent's belief strictly bounded by its physical orientation and sensory limitations, leading to spatial flip errors and hallucinations when agents are out of view.

P — Proposal

The paper introduces the Observe-to-Believe Pipeline, a training-free two-stage framework: Stage I extracts structured visual evidence from Agent A's egocentric stream, and Stage II performs a modality-aware perspective shift via spatial horizon conversion. The key insight is an Anchor-Based Embodied Spatial Decomposition Chain-of-Thought (CoT) that dynamically routes inference through visual or audio pathways based on whether Agent B's visual frustum captures Agent A.

4) Experiments

Main results

The zero-shot baseline accuracy for current MLLMs is 42%. The proposed sensory-bounded reasoning chain robustly outperforms pure egocentric and allocentric baselines, especially in challenging 'invisible' scenarios where agents lack line-of-sight.

Ablation

The dynamic modality-aware routing (switching between audio-visual and audio-only branches) is critical; ablating it causes performance to drop to near-baseline levels in occluded conditions.

Limitations

['The pipeline is training-free and relies on prompt engineering, which may limit scalability to more complex environments.', 'Evaluation is conducted in simulated environments; real-world sensor noise and latency are not addressed.', 'The framework assumes a fixed number of agents and does not handle dynamic, multi-party belief tracking.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses a core limitation of current MLLMs in embodied AI: the inability to model another agent's sensory-constrained beliefs.
  2. Provides a principled, modular approach (geometric-to-semantic projection) that can be integrated into larger embodied systems.
  3. Highlights the importance of explicit epistemic modeling over end-to-end implicit reasoning for multi-agent coordination.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Test the Observe-to-Believe pipeline on real-world robotic platforms with noisy sensors.
  • [ ] Extend the framework to handle more than two agents and dynamic belief updates over time.
  • [ ] Explore fine-tuning MLLMs with the proposed CoT to improve zero-shot spatial ToM reasoning.
