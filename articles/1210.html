AMEL: Accumulated Message Effects on LLM Judgments

AMEL：累积消息对LLM判断的影响

Sid-ali Temkit

Summary

AMEL demonstrates that LLM evaluators are systematically biased by the polarity of prior conversation history, with negative context having a stronger effect, and that this bias saturates quickly and is not mitigated by model scaling.

摘要

本文系统性地揭示了LLM评估者会因对话历史中的主流极性而产生偏见，且负面历史的影响更强。通过大规模实验（75,898次API调用，11个模型），作者量化了这种偏见的大小、饱和点以及模型规模的影响，为生产环境中LLM评估管线的可靠性提供了重要警示。

Deep Note

Deep Note — amel

Key Takeaways - LLM judges shift toward the prevailing polarity of prior conversation history (d=-0.17, p<10^-46). - Bias concentrates on items where the model is uncertain at baseline (d=-0.34 vs d=-0.15 for deterministic items). - Negative history induces 1.62x more bias than positive history (t=13.46, p<10^-39). - Bias saturates after 5 turns; longer contexts do not increase it (Spearman |r|<0.01). - Larger models show less bias but none are immune (e.g., GPT-5.2 d=-0.17 vs Nano d=-0.34).

1) Why-read

AMEL demonstrates that LLM evaluators are systematically biased by the polarity of prior conversation history, with negative context having a stronger effect, and that this bias saturates quickly and is not mitigated by model scaling.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLMs are increasingly used as automated evaluators in production pipelines where multiple items are assessed in a single conversation. Prior work has documented various biases in LLM judgments, including position bias, sycophancy, and majority label bias, but the effect of accumulated prior conversation history on subsequent judgments had not been systematically studied.

R — Related work

  • Zhao et al. (2021) showed few-shot prompting introduces majority label bias and recency bias.
  • Wang et al. (2023) demonstrated strong position bias in LLM evaluators, with order changes altering win rates by up to 75%.
  • Perez et al. (2022) evaluated sycophancy at scale, finding it pervasive across model families.
  • Braun (2024) found LLMs lean toward 'no' in binary judgments, opposite to human acquiescence bias.
  • Chen et al. (2023) showed that in batch relevance assessment, prior document quality shifts the threshold for subsequent judgments.

G — Gap

While individual biases like sycophancy, position bias, and majority label bias have been studied, the cumulative effect of a conversation's prevailing polarity on subsequent LLM judgments had not been empirically characterized across multiple models, domains, and context lengths. The interaction between baseline uncertainty and bias magnitude was also unexplored.

P — Proposal

The paper introduces AMEL (Accumulated Message Effects on LLM Judgments) and systematically measures it across 75,898 API calls to 11 models from 4 providers. The key insight is that presenting identical test items after histories saturated with positive or negative evaluations shifts model responses toward the conversation's prevailing polarity, with the effect concentrated on items where the model is uncertain at baseline.

4) Experiments

Main results

Across 75,898 API calls to 11 models, the overall effect size was d=-0.17 (p<10^-46). For high-entropy items, d=-0.34 vs d=-0.15 for deterministic items. Negative history induced 1.62x more bias than positive (t=13.46, p<10^-39, n=2,481 pairs). Bias saturated at 5 turns (Spearman |r|<0.01, p>0.94). Domain effects: code review d=-0.24, meals d=-0.13, content moderation d=-0.12.

Ablation

Scaling within provider families reduced but did not eliminate bias: Anthropic Haiku d=-0.22 to Opus d=-0.17; OpenAI Nano d=-0.34 to GPT-5.2 d=-0.17. Five biased turns anywhere in a 50-turn history produced the same shift, indicating position independence.

Limitations

['The attribution of negativity asymmetry to token-level vs semantic components is exploratory due to small sample sizes (n=21 items).', 'The study uses synthetic conversation histories; real-world deployment may involve more nuanced interactions.', 'Only binary judgment tasks were tested; effects on continuous or multi-class evaluations are not examined.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly impacts reliability of LLM-as-judge pipelines in production, especially for content moderation and code review.
  2. Negativity asymmetry suggests that models are more susceptible to negative feedback loops, which could amplify errors in sequential evaluation.
  3. Saturation effect implies that even short conversations can introduce bias, making fresh-context recommendations critical.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Implement fresh-context evaluation per item in production pipelines to eliminate AMEL.
  • [ ] When batching is unavoidable, balance the polarity of prior history to mitigate bias.
  • [ ] Investigate whether the negativity asymmetry extends to non-binary judgment formats (e.g., Likert scales).
  • [ ] Explore whether fine-tuning on balanced or neutral histories reduces susceptibility.

主要结论 - LLM评估者的判断会向对话历史的主流极性偏移（效应量d=-0.17，p<10^-46）。 - 偏见主要集中在模型基线不确定的样本上（高熵项d=-0.34 vs 确定性项d=-0.15）。 - 负面历史诱导的偏见是正面历史的1.62倍（t=13.46，p<10^-39）。 - 偏见在5轮对话后达到饱和，更长的上下文不会增加偏见（Spearman |r|<0.01）。 - 更大的模型偏见更小，但没有任何模型完全免疫（例如GPT-5.2 d=-0.17 vs Nano d=-0.34）。

1) 阅读理由

AMEL证明LLM评估者会系统性地受到对话历史极性的偏见影响，且负面上下文效果更强，这种偏见会迅速饱和且无法通过模型规模扩展来消除。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：LLM越来越多地被用作生产管线中的自动评估者，在单个对话中评估多个项目。先前工作已记录了LLM判断中的各种偏见，包括位置偏见、谄媚偏见和多数标签偏见，但累积的对话历史对后续判断的影响尚未被系统研究。

相关工作：Zhao等人（2021）发现少样本提示会引入多数标签偏见和近因偏见；Wang等人（2023）证明了LLM评估者中存在强烈的位置偏见，顺序改变可使胜率变化高达75%；Perez等人（2022）大规模评估了谄媚偏见，发现其在各模型家族中普遍存在；Braun（2024）发现LLM在二元判断中倾向于“否”，与人类的默认肯定偏见相反；Chen等人（2023）表明在批量相关性评估中，先前的文档质量会改变后续判断的阈值。

差距：尽管谄媚、位置偏见和多数标签偏见等个体偏见已被研究，但对话的主流极性对后续LLM判断的累积效应尚未在多个模型、领域和上下文长度下进行实证刻画。基线不确定性与偏见大小之间的相互作用也未被探索。

提案：本文引入了AMEL（累积消息对LLM判断的影响），并通过75,898次API调用（涵盖4家提供商的11个模型）系统地测量了该效应。关键发现是：在充满正面或负面评估的历史之后呈现相同的测试项，会使模型响应向对话的主流极性偏移，且该效应集中在基线不确定的样本上。

3) 实验

在75,898次API调用（11个模型）中，总体效应量为d=-0.17（p<10^-46）。对于高熵项，d=-0.34；对于确定性项，d=-0.15。负面历史诱导的偏见是正面历史的1.62倍（t=13.46，p<10^-39，n=2,481对）。偏见在5轮对话后饱和（Spearman |r|<0.01，p>0.94）。领域效应：代码审查d=-0.24，餐饮d=-0.13，内容审核d=-0.12。

消融实验

在提供商家族内进行模型缩放可减少但无法消除偏见：Anthropic Haiku d=-0.22 到 Opus d=-0.17；OpenAI Nano d=-0.34 到 GPT-5.2 d=-0.17。在50轮历史中任意位置放置5轮有偏见的对话会产生相同的偏移，表明偏见与位置无关。

局限性

['对负面不对称归因于词元级与语义组件的分析是探索性的，因样本量较小（n=21个项）。', '本研究使用合成对话历史；实际部署可能涉及更微妙的交互。', '仅测试了二元判断任务；未检验对连续或多分类评估的影响。']

4) 重要性

  1. 直接影响生产环境中LLM评估管线的可靠性，尤其是内容审核和代码审查场景。
  2. 负面不对称表明模型更容易受到负面反馈循环的影响，可能在顺序评估中放大错误。
  3. 饱和效应意味着即使短对话也能引入偏见，因此为每个项目使用全新上下文至关重要。

5) 下一步

  • [ ] 在生产管线中为每个项目实施全新上下文评估，以消除AMEL。
  • [ ] 当批处理不可避免时，平衡先前历史的极性以减轻偏见。
  • [ ] 研究负面不对称是否扩展到非二元判断格式（如李克特量表）。
