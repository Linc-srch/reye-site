1) Why-read

TeleCom-Bench reveals a universal 'Execution Wall' where LLMs achieve >90% accuracy on linguistic interface tasks but collapse to ~30% on procedural execution tasks like solution generation in telecom.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLMs have been integrated into vertical domains like healthcare and finance, but deployment in telecommunications remains limited due to a lack of standardized evaluation. Existing telecom benchmarks focus on static foundational knowledge and isolated atomic skills, ignoring equipment-specific documentation and end-to-end industrial workflows.

R — Related work

  • SPEC5G and TeleQnA evaluate foundational communication standards and theoretical principles.
  • TeleMath and TeleTables assess isolated mathematical or tabular reasoning skills.
  • TeleLogs and OpsEval explore operational and maintenance tasks but do not capture full task pipelines.

G — Gap

Current telecom benchmarks neglect product-specific documentation and end-to-end industrial workflows, leaving a gap between theoretical understanding and operational reality. They fail to model the complex multi-step reasoning required in real-world production systems, hindering the transition of LLMs from academic prototypes to production-ready telecom agents.

P — Proposal

TeleCom-Bench is a comprehensive benchmark with 12 evaluation sets and 22,678 curated samples, structured around two pillars: Multi-dimensional Knowledge Comprehension (integrating fundamentals, 3GPP protocols, 5G architecture, and proprietary product knowledge via knowledge graph-driven synthesis) and End-to-End Knowledge Application (formalizing six core tasks from live network agent workflows). The key insight is to evaluate LLMs across a synergistic hierarchy from factual recall to complex problem-solving.

3) Figures

4) Experiments

Main results

Evaluations of eight state-of-the-art LLMs show >90% accuracy in intent recognition and entity extraction, but performance drops to approximately 30% in solution generation. Models function competently as diagnosticians but fail as field engineers.

Ablation

The study identifies a universal 'Execution Wall' where performance collapses from linguistic interface tasks to procedural execution tasks, with solution generation accuracy around 30%.

Limitations

['The benchmark is limited to telecommunications domain and may not generalize to other verticals.', 'The evaluation focuses on current LLMs; future models may require updated benchmarks.', 'The dataset relies on proprietary product knowledge from ZTE, which may introduce vendor-specific bias.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Provides a standardized diagnostic to pinpoint the deficit in procedural execution for telecom agents, relevant for building domain-specific LLMs.
  2. Highlights the gap between linguistic understanding and actionable engineering, informing alignment strategies for production-ready systems.
  3. Offers a hierarchical evaluation framework that can be adapted to other industrial domains with similar knowledge application needs.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Investigate fine-tuning or retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) methods to bridge the Execution Wall in telecom procedural tasks.
  • [ ] Extend the benchmark to include more telecom sub-domains (e.g., satellite, optical) and real-time agent interactions.
  • [ ] Explore multi-agent collaboration to decompose complex procedural tasks into manageable subtasks for LLMs.
