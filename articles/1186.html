Finding the molecular switches behind new infectious diseases

Finding the molecular switches behind new infectious diseases

Summary

Google DeepMind's Co-Scientist AI tool is being used by Professor Clare Bryant at the University of Cambridge to identify molecular switches that cause severe infectious diseases like sepsis when pathogens jump from animals to humans. By feeding the tool grant proposals and unpublished data, Bryant's team rapidly generated and refined hypotheses, narrowing down from candidate proteins to specific amino acids for experimental testing. This process, which would normally take two to three years, is now expected to be completed in six months, demonstrating the potential of AI to accelerate infectious disease research by prioritizing the most promising targets.

摘要

Google DeepMind的Co-Scientist AI工具正被剑桥大学的Clare Bryant教授用于识别病原体从动物传播到人类时引发严重传染病（如败血症）的分子开关。通过输入基金提案和未发表数据，团队快速生成并优化假设，从候选蛋白缩小到特定氨基酸进行实验验证。这一通常需两到三年的过程现预计六个月内完成，展示了AI通过优先排序最有前景靶点来加速传染病研究的潜力。
