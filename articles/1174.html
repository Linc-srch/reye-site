Auditing Privacy in Multi-Tenant RAG under Account Collusion

审计多租户RAG中的账户合谋隐私泄露

content-management rlhf supply-chain-attack

Florian A. D. Burnat, Brittany I. Davidson

启示 · Relevance

This finding directly undermines the privacy guarantees of multi-tenant RAG systems, which are increasingly used to augment autonomous agents; your research should prioritize auditing and red-teaming these systems for collusion-based leakage as a new, high-impact attack vector in agent governance.

该发现直接削弱了多租户RAG系统的隐私保证，而这类系统正越来越多地用于增强自主智能体；您的研究应优先审计和红队测试这些系统在共谋下的信息泄露，将其作为智能体治理中一个高影响力的新型攻击向量。

Summary

Key claim: Per-account DP guarantees in multi-tenant RAG structurally understate joint leakage under account collusion; key observation: the degradation rate is Θ(√k·ε_acc) and is empirically verifiable via a novel audit protocol.

摘要

本文揭示了多租户RAG系统中，多个账户合谋会系统性削弱单账户差分隐私保证，退化率为Θ(√k·ε_acc)。作者设计并验证了首个无需修改部署的审计协议，能够针对检索分数通道发布量化(PASS, ε_audit)判决，填补了理论与实际威胁模型之间的空白。

Deep Note

Deep Note — auditing-rag-collusion

Key Takeaways - Same-index multi-account collusion in multi-tenant RAG causes joint leakage that degrades at rate Θ(√k·ε_acc) for Gaussian-noised retrieval, matching DP composition theory. - Cross-tenant and external collusion have zero leakage by design unless access control fails (M4), making them architectural audits rather than DP audits. - The paper designs the first audit protocol for unmodified RAG deployments that outputs a quantitative (PASS, ε_audit) verdict for the retrieval-score channel without index disclosure or pipeline redesign. - Empirical membership-inference attacks confirm the √k scaling in both scalar and top-K settings, with real embedders (bge-small-en-v1.5) preserving the rate despite a constant-factor degradation. - Production-scale HNSW experiments (10^6 passages) show user-observable AUC remains at chance, while auditor-observable pooled noisy-score preserves the monotone curve, highlighting the need for audit attestations.

1) Why-read

This paper identifies same-index multi-account collusion as a privacy-boundary failure in multi-tenant RAG and provides the first audit protocol to quantitatively detect joint leakage without modifying the deployment.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Multi-tenant retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) services claim per-account differential privacy as the leakage boundary, but standard DP composition theory suggests that multiple accounts colluding against the same index can amplify leakage. Prior work has not addressed the practical auditability of this gap in real RAG systems.

R — Related work

  • Differential privacy composition theorems (e.g., moments accountant, Rényi DP) that predict leakage growth with number of queries.
  • Membership inference attacks (MIAs) against DP mechanisms, including those targeting RAG retrieval.
  • Audit protocols for DP systems that require model-weight exposure or pipeline redesign.
  • Multi-tenant architectures in machine learning and database systems with access-control boundaries.

G — Gap

Existing privacy audits for RAG either assume a single-account setting or require full index disclosure and pipeline modifications. No prior work audits the per-account vs. joint leakage gap under same-index multi-account collusion, nor provides a protocol that works against unmodified deployments.

P — Proposal

The paper formalizes same-index multi-account collusion as a privacy-boundary failure and derives a RAG-specific membership inference attack (MIA) that achieves the Θ(√k·ε_acc) rate. It then designs an audit protocol combining generic cryptographic primitives (Merkle ledgers, ZK proofs, Gaussian noise attestations) with six RAG-specific primitives to issue a quantitative (PASS, ε_audit) verdict for the retrieval-score channel without index disclosure or pipeline changes.

4) Experiments

Main results

Empirical membership-inference AUC against coalition size k matches the predicted 1/2 + 1/2 Φ(√k ε_acc / (4√(log(1/δ_acc) log(1.25/δ_q)))) within one standard error for the scalar mechanism at n=10,000 queries, ε_acc∈{1,2,4}, T=10,000 trials. For the full top-K harness (K=5, 50 background documents, embedding dimension 32, n=200, T=2,000), the √k scaling is preserved with a constant-factor degradation at looser ε_acc∈{4,8,16}. Real embedder (bge-small-en-v1.5, 384-dim) experiments show a 4.84× advantage across k∈{1,2,5,10,20} against a predicted √20≈4.47, confirming the rate.

Ablation

Alternative adversaries (Bayes LR, diversified split at ρ=0.50 and ρ=0.25) trail the baseline at every k>1, and no alternative adversary exceeds the √k rate. External vs. same-tenant collusion curves overlap within 0.022 AUC (median 0.010), confirming that cross-tenant leakage is negligible without access-control failure.

Limitations

['The audit protocol only covers the retrieval-score channel; generation-channel privacy (LLM output) is explicitly scoped out and requires separate auditing.', 'Production-scale HNSW experiments (10^6 passages) show user-observable AUC remains at chance, meaning the attack may not be detectable by end users without auditor-observable attestations.', 'The protocol assumes honest-but-curious auditor and requires deployment of cryptographic primitives (Merkle ledgers, ZK proofs) which may introduce overhead.']

5) Why it matters

  1. For researchers building multi-tenant RAG systems, this work highlights that per-account DP guarantees are insufficient when accounts can collude; joint leakage scales with √k.
  2. The audit protocol provides a practical blueprint for verifying privacy boundaries without modifying existing RAG pipelines, which is critical for real-world deployment.
  3. The empirical confirmation of √k scaling with real embedders (bge-small-en-v1.5) and production-scale HNSW indexes grounds theoretical DP composition in practical RAG settings.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Extend the audit protocol to cover the generation channel (LLM output) and compose with retrieval-channel audits.
  • [ ] Implement the cryptographic primitives (Merkle ledgers, ZK proofs) in a real RAG service to measure overhead and practicality.
  • [ ] Investigate mitigation strategies (e.g., per-account noise calibration, coalition detection) to bound joint leakage without sacrificing utility.
  • [ ] Test the attack and audit on larger, more diverse embedding models and index structures (e.g., IVF, product quantization).

深度笔记

审计多租户RAG中的账户合谋隐私泄露

主要结论 - 相同索引的多账户合谋使高斯噪声检索的每账户DP保证以Θ(√k·ε_acc)速率退化。 - 首个针对未修改多租户RAG部署的审计协议，可发布检索分数通道的量化(PASS, ε_audit)判决。 - 实验验证了成员推断AUC随k呈√k增长趋势，并通过了两个伪造门测试。 - 协议将通用密码学原语（Merkle账本、ZK证明）与六个RAG特定原语组合，无需泄露索引。 - 发现并纠正了朴素多账户组合中的δ核算错误，要求δ_policy > k_max·δ_acc。

1) 阅读理由

核心主张：多租户RAG中的每账户DP保证在账户合谋下系统性低估了联合泄露；关键发现：退化率为Θ(√k·ε_acc)，且可通过新型审计协议进行实证验证。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：生产级RAG服务（如Microsoft 365 Copilot、OpenAI Assistants）为多租户架构，并宣称提供每账户差分隐私。现有成员推断攻击将单一攻击者作为分析单元，但多账户滥用是操作现实。欧盟AI法案等监管框架要求在不暴露模型或数据的情况下进行可审计披露。

相关工作：单攻击者RAG MIA（Naseh2025-riddle等）假设k=1且API无状态；联邦学习合谋（Lyu2023-cerberus等）在训练时针对梯度聚合建模协调；DP-RAG防御（Cheng2025-remoterag）修改部署以减少每次查询泄露，本文保持部署不变；DP组合理论（DworkRothblumVadhan2010等）提供了速率但未涉及威胁模型。

差距：现有每账户DP保证假设单一攻击者（k=1），但生产RAG账户成本低廉，攻击者可汇集多个账户。尚无工作形式化相同索引多账户合谋下的隐私退化，或提供针对未修改部署且无需索引泄露的审计协议。

提案：本文定义了多租户RAG的(ε,δ,k)-合谋DP，并证明了高斯噪声检索下严格的Θ(√k·ε_acc)退化率。设计了验证者可运行的审计协议，使用密码学原语（Merkle账本、ZK证明、高斯噪声证明）和六个RAG特定原语，在不修改部署的情况下为检索分数通道发布(PASS, ε_audit)判决。

3) 实验

主要结果：在基于FAISS的合成多租户测试平台上，实证AUC随k变化的曲线确认了√k趋势：在固定每账户预算下，AUC = 1/2 + Θ(√k·ε_acc)。该趋势在top-K后处理和训练嵌入器下依然成立。通过了两个伪造门测试（斜率门和紧致门）以及一个诊断测试（尺度崩溃）。

消融实验

消融实验确认√k缩放关系在top-K后处理和训练嵌入器下成立；替代攻击者的鲁棒性也得到验证。Rényi-DP矩会计在中等k下比闭式界给出更紧的(ε,δ)对。

局限性

['生成通道隐私（LLM基于选定文档的输出）被明确排除在外，仅审计检索分数通道。', '协议假设提供者诚实遵循承诺阶段；恶意提供者可能在证明前偏离。', '实证验证基于合成部署；真实生产RAG服务可能存在额外复杂性（如缓存、速率限制规避）。']

4) 重要性

  1. 直接针对生产RAG服务操作相关的现实威胁模型（多账户合谋）。
  2. 提供符合监管要求（EU AI法案、DSA）的实用审计协议，无需数据披露即可进行第三方审计。
  3. 通过推导并验证RAG特定的MIA预测，弥合了DP理论与实证MIA之间的差距。
  4. 强调多账户滥用不仅是欺诈问题，更是隐私边界失效，重新定义了操作实践。
