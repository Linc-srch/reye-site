TrapDoor Supply Chain Attack Spreads Credential-Stealing Malware via npm, PyPI, and CratesIO

TrapDoor Supply Chain Attack Spreads Credential-Stealing Malware via npm, PyPI, and CratesIO

content-management supply-chain-attack zero-trust
Summary

A coordinated supply chain attack named TrapDoor has targeted npm, PyPI, and Crates.io with over 34 malicious packages across 384 versions, aiming to steal credentials, crypto wallets, SSH keys, and cloud credentials from developers in crypto, DeFi, Solana, and AI communities. The malware uses ecosystem-specific execution paths, including postinstall hooks, remote JavaScript payloads, and malicious build.rs scripts. Notably, it implants hidden instructions in .cursorrules and CLAUDE.md files to trick AI assistants into exfiltrating secrets. The campaign also opens GitHub PRs on popular AI projects to propagate malicious files, demonstrating a novel blend of typosquatting and developer-environment attacks.

摘要

名为TrapDoor的协同供应链攻击针对npm、PyPI和Crates.io，发布了超过34个恶意包（共384个版本），旨在窃取加密货币、DeFi、Solana和AI领域开发者的凭证、加密钱包、SSH密钥和云凭证。恶意软件利用生态系统特定的执行路径，包括postinstall钩子、远程JavaScript载荷和恶意build.rs脚本。值得注意的是，它在.cursorrules和CLAUDE.md文件中植入隐藏指令，诱骗AI助手泄露机密。该活动还向热门AI项目发起GitHub PR以传播恶意文件，展示了typosquatting与开发者环境攻击的新型结合。
