Making Vulnerable Drivers Exploitable Without Hardware - The BYOVD Perspective

Making Vulnerable Drivers Exploitable Without Hardware - The BYOVD Perspective

Summary

The article explores how vulnerable Windows kernel drivers can be exploited without the required hardware, focusing on the Bring Your Own Vulnerable Driver (BYOVD) attack technique. It analyzes device object creation patterns in drivers, distinguishing between unconditional creation upon load and conditional creation tied to hardware presence or registry keys. The author demonstrates that many PnP-compatible drivers require specific setup to trigger AddDevice and other initialization routines, making hardware-gating a key obstacle. Techniques to bypass hardware dependencies solely from userland are discussed, emphasizing the relevance for post-exploitation attacks that aim to disable security components like EDR. The work provides a methodology for evaluating driver vulnerability reachability.

摘要

本文探讨了如何在没有所需硬件的情况下利用有漏洞的Windows内核驱动程序，重点介绍了BYOVD（Bring Your Own Vulnerable Driver）攻击技术。分析了驱动程序中设备对象的创建模式，区分了加载时的无条件创建和与硬件存在或注册表键相关的条件创建。作者证明了许多PnP兼容驱动程序需要特定设置才能触发AddDevice等初始化例程，使得硬件门控成为关键障碍。讨论了仅从用户态绕过硬件依赖的方法，强调了对后利用攻击（如禁用EDR安全组件）的相关性。该工作提供了一种评估驱动程序漏洞可达性的方法论。
