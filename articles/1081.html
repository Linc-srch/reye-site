1) Why-read

2) CRGP

C — Context

Post-training quantization (PTQ) compresses large models without retraining, but low-bit settings suffer from error accumulation across layers. Residual reconstruction methods like GPTAQ and ResComp introduce cross-layer residuals to mitigate this, yet they rely on a Hessian-approximation (HA) assumption that can introduce bias. This paper identifies that the trade-off between error correction and HA bias is module-dependent, motivating adaptive per-module control.

R — Related work

  • GPTQ (Frantar et al.) extends OBS/OBQ for layer-wise Hessian-based weight updates in PTQ.
  • GPTAQ (Liu et al.) and ResComp (Wang et al.) add cross-layer activation residuals to GPTQ to reduce accumulated quantization error.
  • Quantization-parameter methods (e.g., clipping range optimization) and redistribution methods (e.g., SmoothQuant, QuaRot) improve low-bit robustness from other angles.

G — Gap

Existing residual reconstruction methods apply a single global residual strength across all modules, ignoring that the optimal balance between accumulated-error correction and Hessian-approximation bias varies per module. This leads to suboptimal quantization, especially under very low-bit settings (≤4-bit), where the HA bias can dominate.

P — Proposal

MARR introduces a module-specific scaling coefficient for the residual term in each module's reconstruction objective, adaptively controlling the injected cross-layer information. To avoid expensive search, it uses a PID-based feedback controller that updates the coefficient based on the module's reconstruction error, progressively refining the balance.

4) Experiments

Main results

On LLMs (Llama2-7b, Llama3-8b, OPT-6.7b) under W2A4 quantization, MARR reduces perplexity on WikiText2 by up to 14.94% (Llama2-7b) and 9.82% (Llama3-8b) over GPTAQ. On ViTs (DeiT-S, ViT-B) under 4-bit weight quantization, it achieves up to 4.6% relative accuracy gains over ResComp.

Ablation

Ablation on the PID controller shows that using all three terms (P, I, D) yields the best perplexity (e.g., 8.23 on Llama2-7b W2A4), while removing the integral term degrades performance to 8.45, and removing derivative to 8.31, confirming the necessity of each component.

Limitations

['The PID-based update introduces additional hyperparameters (Kp, Ki, Kd) that may require tuning for different model families.', 'The method is evaluated only on linear and attention modules; applicability to other layer types (e.g., convolutions) is not explored.', 'The scaling coefficient is applied per module, which increases the number of learned parameters compared to global scaling, though the PID update avoids exhaustive search.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Provides a principled way to handle the two-sided effect of residual reconstruction, directly addressing a core limitation of state-of-the-art PTQ methods.
  2. The PID-based adaptive update is a novel application of control theory to quantization, offering a lightweight alternative to brute-force hyperparameter search.
  3. Achieves significant gains in very low-bit regimes (2-bit weights, 4-bit activations), which are critical for deploying large models on resource-constrained hardware.
  4. The module-adaptive insight may generalize to other reconstruction-based compression techniques (e.g., pruning, distillation) where per-module trade-offs exist.
