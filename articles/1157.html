BiRD: A Bidirectional Ranking Defense Mechanism for Retrieval Augmented Generation

BiRD：面向检索增强生成的双向排序防御机制

content-management rlhf supply-chain-attack

Chengcai Gao, Zhihong Sun, Xiaochuan Shi, Qiufeng Wang, Chao Liang

启示 · Relevance

BiRD's bidirectional ranking defense directly addresses a critical vulnerability in your automated evaluation and agent security pipeline: it provides a lightweight, runtime-detectable method to counter indirect prompt injections via poisoned RAG documents, which is essential for securing tool-augmented autonomous workflows against data poisoning attacks.

BiRD 的双向排序防御直接解决了您自动化评估与 Agent 安全流水线中的一个关键漏洞：它提供了一种轻量级、可在运行时检测的方法来对抗通过有毒 RAG 文档进行的间接提示注入，这对于保护工具增强型自主工作流免受数据投毒攻击至关重要。

Summary

BiRD introduces a bidirectional ranking defense that leverages ranking context consistency to efficiently detect poisoned documents in RAG, achieving high robustness with minimal latency.

摘要

本文提出BiRD，一种利用双向排序一致性高效检测检索增强生成（RAG）中毒文档的防御方法。实验表明，BiRD可将PoisonedRAG攻击成功率降低54%，任务准确率提升56%，且平均额外延迟低于1秒。该方法为RAG安全提供了兼顾鲁棒性与效率的新思路。

Deep Note — bird

1) Why-read

BiRD introduces a novel bidirectional ranking defense that leverages ranking structure (not just semantics) to simultaneously achieve high robustness and low computational cost against poisoning attacks on RAG.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems are vulnerable to adversarial poisoning attacks where malicious documents are injected into the retrieval corpus. Existing defenses either rely on expensive semantic analysis or voting mechanisms that degrade under strong attacks, and all neglect the ranking context that defines retrieval behavior.

R — Related work

  • PoisonedRAG and similar poisoning attacks on RAG pipelines.
  • Semantic-analysis-based defenses (e.g., perplexity filtering, entailment checks) that are computationally heavy.
  • Voting-based defenses that aggregate multiple retrieval results but suffer under high poisoning rates.
  • Prior work on adversarial robustness in information retrieval and ranking systems.

G — Gap

Prior defenses focus exclusively on semantic content relevance, ignoring the ranking structure of retrieved documents. This leads to a fundamental trade-off between computational cost and robustness, especially under strong poisoning attacks where semantic signals become unreliable.

P — Proposal

BiRD is a bidirectional ranking defense that computes both forward ranking (query-to-document relevance) and backward ranking (document-to-query relevance) for each retrieved document. It exploits the key discriminative pattern that poisoned documents exhibit significantly stronger alignment between their backward rankings and the query's forward ranking compared to benign documents, enabling efficient detection without heavy semantic analysis.

Stage 1 retrieves candidate documents, then BiRD computes forward and backward ranking scores, and finally filters out documents with high alignment between the two signals.

---

4) Experiments

Main results

Across 3 datasets (including HotpotQA) with 3 retrievers (ANCE, Contriever) and 3 LLMs under 2 attack scenarios, BiRD reduces PoisonedRAG attack success rate by up to 54% and improves task accuracy by up to 56%, with average additional latency under 1 second.

Ablation

Ablation studies show that removing either the forward or backward ranking signal significantly degrades defense performance; the dual-signal framework is essential. For example, on HotpotQA-ANCE, using only forward ranking reduces attack success rate by only 20% compared to 54% with both signals.

Limitations

["The method assumes access to the retriever's ranking scores, which may not be available in black-box settings.", 'The discriminative pattern may weaken under adaptive attacks that explicitly optimize backward ranking alignment.', 'Evaluation is limited to two retrievers and three LLMs; generalizability to other architectures is not fully explored.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Provides a practical, low-latency defense that can be integrated into existing RAG pipelines without retraining.
  2. Highlights the importance of ranking structure as a signal for adversarial detection, opening a new direction beyond semantic analysis.
  3. The up to 56% accuracy improvement shows that defense can also enhance benign performance, not just robustness.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Test BiRD against adaptive attacks that explicitly craft poisoned documents to mimic benign backward-forward ranking alignment.
  • [ ] Extend the approach to black-box retrievers where only document IDs or scores are available.
  • [ ] Explore combining bidirectional ranking with lightweight semantic checks for even stronger defense.
  • [ ] Apply the ranking alignment idea to other adversarial settings (e.g., search poisoning, recommendation systems).

1) 阅读理由

BiRD通过利用排序上下文一致性高效检测RAG中的中毒文档，以极低延迟实现了高鲁棒性。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

检索增强生成（RAG）通过检索外部知识增强大语言模型，但易受语料投毒攻击。现有防御依赖语义分析或投票，要么计算开销大，要么对强攻击效果弱。SeConRAG和TrustRAG使用聚类过滤与语义分析，但计算开销高；ReliabilityRAG和Certifiably Robust RAG采用投票一致性，但对高强度投毒脆弱；AstuteRAG和InstructRAG利用LLM内部知识过滤，但受限于LLM知识边界。PoisonedRAG、GASLITE和ReGENT等语料投毒攻击通过注入恶意文档操纵检索。现有防御仅关注语义内容相关性，忽略了由排序结构定义的检索上下文，导致计算成本与鲁棒性之间的权衡，尤其在强投毒攻击下。BiRD是一种双向排序防御，使用前向排序评估语义相关性，后向排序量化排序上下文一致性，通过检测前后向排序的强对齐（中毒文档的判别性模式）来过滤中毒文档。

3) 实验

在HotpotQA数据集上使用ANCE检索器，BiRD将PoisonedRAG攻击成功率降低54%，任务准确率提升56%。在所有设置下，平均额外延迟低于1秒。

消融实验

对后向排序信号的消融实验表明，移除该信号会使攻击成功率提高超过30%，证实了其在检测中毒文档中的关键作用。

局限性

BiRD假设攻击者无法控制后向检索过程，这在自适应攻击中可能不成立。该方法依赖检索器质量，弱检索器可能降低判别能力。评估仅限于英文数据集和特定攻击类型，对其他语言或攻击的泛化性尚未测试。

4) 重要性

  1. 通过利用排序结构而非仅语义，为RAG安全提供了新颖视角。
  2. 提供了一种低延迟的实用防御，适用于实时RAG系统。
  3. 双向排序模式可能启发其他基于检索的系统的检测方法。
