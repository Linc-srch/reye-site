1) Why-read

Mind Dreamer proposes Active Latent Intervention (ALI) to overcome the Historical Tethering limitation in MBRL by synthesizing non-continuous latent jumps, achieving up to 8.8x speedup on sparse-reward tasks.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Model-Based Reinforcement Learning (MBRL) uses latent imagination for sample efficiency, but imagination is typically initialized only from observed states in a replay buffer. This creates a learning asymmetry where the world model's manifold discovery outpaces the policy's sparse-reward optimization, confining exploration to previously traversed regions.

R — Related work

  • Plan2Explore optimizes global curiosity but still initializes rollouts from the replay buffer.
  • Hindsight Experience Replay (HER) and Hierarchical RL excel at trajectory exploitation but remain 'prisoners of the buffer'.
  • Go-Explore requires explicit environment resets to known states, unlike MD's mental teleportation.
  • Successor Representations decouple dynamics from rewards but require continuous discovery.

G — Gap

Prior exploration methods (intrinsic motivation, goal-relabeling) remain trajectory-bound and cannot reason beyond the convex hull of past experiences. No existing work addresses the credit assignment paradox when performing non-continuous latent jumps across spatial ruptures in the manifold.

P — Proposal

Mind Dreamer (MD) uses an adversarial generator to sample initial states from a learned distribution p_gen, enabling non-continuous latent jumps to epistemic blind spots. It derives Relay Value Function (RVF) and Relay Uncertainty Function (RUF) to propagate pragmatic and epistemic value across discontinuities, with a quadratic discount γ^2 for stable uncertainty propagation.

4) Experiments

Main results

On DeepMind Control Suite, MD achieves 1.67x average speedup over DreamerV3, reaching 8.8x in sparse-reward tasks.

Ablation

The quadratic discount γ^2 is critical for stable uncertainty propagation across latent jumps; without it, the generator chases distal hallucinations.

Limitations

['The adversarial generator may produce physically implausible states if the world model is inaccurate.', 'The quadratic discount γ^2 may overly constrain exploration in environments with very long horizons.', 'The framework assumes a well-learned latent manifold, which may not hold in highly stochastic or partially observable domains.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses the exploration bottleneck in sparse-reward environments, a key challenge in your research on sample-efficient RL.
  2. The theoretical connection to spectral gap expansion and variance-minimizing importance sampling provides a principled foundation for manifold repair.
  3. The concept of Active Latent Intervention could be extended to other domains like goal-conditioned RL or hierarchical planning.
