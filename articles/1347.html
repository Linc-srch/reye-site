JINX-0164 Targets Cryptocurrency Firms with Fake Recruiter Lures and macOS Malware

JINX-0164 Targets Cryptocurrency Firms with Fake Recruiter Lures and macOS Malware

supply-chain-attack Malware & Threats social-engineering
Summary

A previously undocumented threat actor, JINX-0164, targets cryptocurrency firms using recruitment-themed social engineering and custom macOS malware. Active since mid-2025, the group uses fake LinkedIn profiles and meeting invites to trick victims into installing a Python-based infostealer and remote access trojan called AUDIOFIX. The malware steals credentials, cryptocurrency wallet data, and SSH keys, and enables lateral movement to CI/CD infrastructure. In one case, a supply chain attack was executed. The actor also distributes a Go-based backdoor named MiniRAT via a compromised npm package. While tactics resemble North Korean groups, no direct infrastructure overlaps have been found.

摘要

一个名为JINX-0164的未知威胁行为者利用招聘主题的社会工程学和定制macOS恶意软件攻击加密货币公司。自2025年中活跃以来，该组织通过虚假LinkedIn资料和会议邀请诱骗受害者安装基于Python的信息窃取器和远程访问木马AUDIOFIX。该恶意软件窃取凭证、加密货币钱包数据和SSH密钥，并横向移动到CI/CD基础设施。在至少一个案例中，实施了供应链攻击。该行为者还通过被篡改的npm包分发名为MiniRAT的Go后门。虽然战术与朝鲜黑客组织相似，但尚未发现直接的基础设施重叠。
