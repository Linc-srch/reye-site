Chinese APTs Share Linux Backdoor in Central Asia Telco Attacks

Chinese APTs Share Linux Backdoor in Central Asia Telco Attacks

content-management supply-chain-attack zero-trust
Summary

Chinese state-aligned hackers have been using a Linux backdoor called 'Showboat' (also known as 'kworker') to spy on telecommunications companies in Central Asia and beyond. Discovered by Black Lotus Labs, Showboat is a post-exploitation framework that can scan and infect devices on a local area network not connected to the public internet. Multiple Chinese APT groups, including Calypso (Red Lamassu), have shared and used this tool against targets in Afghanistan, Kazakhstan, Turkey, and Ukraine. Despite being less sophisticated than other malware like BPFdoor, Showboat has remained undetected by VirusTotal and has been active since at least mid-2022, suggesting its simplicity is a deliberate design choice for smaller markets.

摘要

中国国家支持的APT组织使用名为'Showboat'（也称'kworker'）的Linux后门框架，长期监控中亚及周边地区的电信公司。该工具由Black Lotus Labs发现，能够扫描并感染未连接公共互联网的局域网设备。包括Calypso（Red Lamassu）在内的多个中国APT组织共享了此工具，针对阿富汗、哈萨克斯坦、土耳其和乌克兰的目标。尽管Showboat的复杂度低于BPFdoor等恶意软件，但自2022年中以来未被VirusTotal检测到，其简单性被视为针对小市场的刻意设计。
