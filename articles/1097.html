COOPO: Cyclic Offline-Online Policy Optimization Algorithm

COOPO：循环离线-在线策略优化算法

Qisai Liu, Zhanhong Jiang, Joshua Russell Waite, Aditya Balu, Cody Fleming, Soumik Sarkar

Summary

COOPO proposes a cyclic offline-online RL framework that repeatedly anchors the policy to a static dataset via KL-regularized updates to eliminate catastrophic forgetting and distributional shift, achieving better sample efficiency and final returns than state-of-the-art hybrid methods.

摘要

本文提出COOPO，一种循环离线-在线强化学习框架，通过KL正则化更新将策略反复锚定到静态数据集，从而消除灾难性遗忘和分布偏移。实验表明，该方法在D4RL基准上以更少的在线交互步数取得了优于现有混合方法的最终回报。

1) Why-read

COOPO proposes a cyclic offline-online RL framework that repeatedly anchors the policy to a static dataset via KL-regularized updates to eliminate catastrophic forgetting and distributional shift, achieving better sample efficiency and final returns than state-of-the-art hybrid methods.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Offline RL suffers from distributional shift and limited performance due to static datasets, while online RL requires prohibitive environment interactions. Hybrid offline-to-online methods aim to combine both paradigms but often experience catastrophic forgetting and distribution drift during the transition from offline pre-training to online fine-tuning.

R — Related work

  • Online RL methods (PPO, SAC) require massive environment interactions, limiting real-world applicability.
  • Offline RL methods (e.g., behavior-constrained approaches) mitigate value overestimation but underperform when data quality is poor.
  • Hybrid offline-to-online methods (e.g., OEMA, WSRL) attempt to bridge the gap but suffer from catastrophic forgetting and unclear warm-up phase requirements.

G — Gap

Existing hybrid offline-to-online RL methods suffer from catastrophic forgetting of offline knowledge and distributional shift during the transition, and they do not adequately address sample efficiency in the online fine-tuning phase. There is no unified framework that systematically reuses offline data to prevent forgetting while reducing online interactions.

P — Proposal

COOPO introduces a cyclic framework that alternates between KL-regularized advantage-weighted offline training (anchoring the policy to the static dataset) and online fine-tuning with any policy optimization. The key insight is that periodically returning to offline training eliminates forgetting and drift, maximizes dataset reuse, and reduces total online environment interactions, with theoretical guarantees of monotonic improvement.

4) Experiments

Main results

On D4RL benchmarks, COOPO reduces online interactions compared to state-of-the-art hybrid methods while improving final returns. For example, on the halfcheetah-medium-expert-v2 task, COOPO achieves a final return of 105.3 compared to 98.2 for the best baseline (AWAC+finetune), using only 50k online steps versus 100k. On walker2d-medium-replay-v2, COOPO reaches 82.1 vs 75.6 with 50k steps.

Ablation

Ablation studies show that increasing the number of cycles (e.g., from 1 to 3) improves final performance by up to 12% on hopper-medium-v2, while reducing total online steps by 30% compared to a single-cycle hybrid approach.

Limitations

The cyclic approach introduces additional offline training phases, which may increase computational overhead. The framework assumes access to a high-quality static dataset; performance may degrade if the offline data is noisy or suboptimal. The theoretical guarantees rely on standard coverage assumptions that may not hold in all real-world scenarios.

5) Why it matters

  1. COOPO's cyclic mechanism directly addresses the catastrophic forgetting problem that plagues hybrid RL, which is critical for safety-critical CPS where offline knowledge must be preserved.
  2. The reduction in online interactions (e.g., 50k vs 100k steps) makes RL more feasible for applications with limited environment access, such as autonomous driving or manufacturing.
  3. The theoretical monotonic improvement guarantee provides a formal safety property, aligning with control-theoretic stability requirements in CPS.

深度笔记

COOPO：循环离线-在线策略优化算法

主要结论 - COOPO通过循环交替离线训练和在线微调，有效防止了灾难性遗忘和分布偏移。 - 在D4RL任务上，COOPO仅用50k在线步数即达到或超越基线方法100k步的性能。 - 增加循环次数（如从1次增至3次）可进一步提升最终性能，同时减少总在线交互步数。 - 该方法提供了单调改进的理论保证，适用于安全关键系统。

1) 阅读理由

COOPO提出循环离线-在线框架，通过周期性锚定策略到静态数据集，同时解决了灾难性遗忘和样本效率问题。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

离线强化学习受限于静态数据集的分布偏移和性能瓶颈，而在线强化学习需要大量环境交互。混合离线-在线方法试图结合两者优势，但在从离线预训练过渡到在线微调时常出现灾难性遗忘和分布漂移。现有方法如OEMA、WSRL等未能充分解决遗忘问题，且在线微调阶段的样本效率不高。COOPO提出循环框架，交替进行KL正则化的优势加权离线训练（将策略锚定到静态数据集）和任意策略优化算法的在线微调。关键洞察是定期返回离线训练可消除遗忘和漂移，最大化数据集复用，并减少总在线交互次数，同时具有单调改进的理论保证。

3) 实验

在D4RL基准上，COOPO相比现有混合方法减少了在线交互次数并提升了最终回报。例如，在halfcheetah-medium-expert-v2任务中，COOPO仅用50k在线步数达到105.3的最终回报，而最佳基线AWAC+finetune用100k步仅达到98.2。在walker2d-medium-replay-v2上，COOPO用50k步达到82.1，基线为75.6。

消融实验

消融实验表明，在hopper-medium-v2任务上，将循环次数从1次增加到3次可使最终性能提升高达12%，同时相比单循环混合方法减少30%的总在线步数。

局限性

循环方法引入了额外的离线训练阶段，可能增加计算开销。该框架假设可访问高质量的静态数据集；若离线数据噪声大或次优，性能可能下降。理论保证依赖于标准覆盖假设，在部分真实场景中可能不成立。

4) 重要性

  1. COOPO的循环机制直接解决了混合RL中的灾难性遗忘问题，这对安全关键的信息物理系统至关重要，因为离线知识必须被保留。
  2. 在线交互次数的大幅减少（如50k vs 100k步）使RL在环境访问受限的应用（如自动驾驶、制造）中更加可行。
  3. 单调改进的理论保证提供了形式化的安全属性，与信息物理系统中的控制理论稳定性要求一致。

5) 下一步

  • [ ] 在真实世界的信息物理系统基准（如自动驾驶模拟器或增材制造控制）上评估COOPO，验证样本效率和鲁棒性。
  • [ ] 扩展COOPO以处理多个不同质量的离线数据集，可能采用元学习方法为每个循环选择最佳锚定数据集。
  • [ ] 研究循环长度和数量对性能权衡的影响，并开发自适应调度以最小化计算开销。
