Beyond Acoustic Emotion Recognition: Multimodal Pathos Analysis in Political Speech Using LLM-Based and Acoustic Emotion Models

超越声学情感识别：基于LLM和多模态情感模型的政治演讲悲情分析

Juergen Dietrich

Summary

This paper demonstrates that LLM-based multimodal analysis captures politically relevant Pathos substantially better than acoustic emotion recognition alone, using a case study of a Bundestag speech.

摘要

本文通过分析德国联邦议院演讲片段，证明基于LLM的多模态分析（Gemini）在捕捉政治相关悲情方面显著优于纯声学情感识别（emotion2vec）。研究揭示了标准声学情感识别基准（如EMO-DB）在自然政治演讲中的生态效度局限，为政治话语情感分析提供了新方法论。

Deep Note

Deep Note — multimodal-pathos

Key Takeaways - LLM-based multimodal analysis (Gemini) correlates significantly with TRUST-Pathos scores (rho=+0.664, p<0.001), while acoustic SER (emotion2vec) does not (rho=+0.097, p=0.499). - Standard SER benchmarks like EMO-DB suffer from acted speech, cultural bias, and category incompatibility, limiting ecological validity for naturalistic political speech. - Acoustic features remain useful for low-level Arousal estimation but fail to capture semantically defined political emotion. - Post-hoc Russell Circumplex projection from discrete SER class probabilities relies on unverified assumptions and may not transfer to naturalistic political speech.

1) Why-read

This paper demonstrates that LLM-based multimodal analysis captures politically relevant Pathos substantially better than acoustic emotion recognition alone, using a case study of a Bundestag speech.

2) CRGP

C — Context

The TRUST framework evaluates political speech along Logos, Ethos, and Pathos dimensions. While Logos and Ethos can be assessed via fact-checking and credibility scoring, Pathos requires recognizing affective states in lexical content, prosody, and rhetoric. Acoustic SER models have advanced on acted benchmarks but their validity for naturalistic political speech is unexplored.

R — Related work

  • Speech Emotion Recognition (SER) benchmarks like EMO-DB and IEMOCAP dominate the field but rely on acted speech with fixed sentences.
  • LLMs like Gemini show competence in understanding emotional nuance from audio and text in open-ended paradigms.
  • The TRUST pipeline operationalizes Pathos as societal impact of emotional language on a -2 to +2 scale.

G — Gap

Acoustic SER models are typically evaluated on acted corpora with forced-choice categories, which may not generalize to naturalistic political speech. The specific limitation addressed is whether acoustic emotion features can serve as valid proxies for the politically relevant Pathos dimension, compared to LLM-based multimodal analysis.

P — Proposal

The authors compare emotion2vec acoustic features, Gemini multimodal analysis, and TRUST-Pathos scores on 51 segments of a Bundestag speech. They introduce post-hoc Russell Circumplex projection to derive continuous Arousal/Valence from discrete SER class probabilities, and evaluate EMO-DB using Gemini as an open-ended annotator to reveal structural limitations.

4) Experiments

Main results

On 41 valid segments of a Banaszak speech, Gemini Valence correlates with TRUST-Pathos at rho=+0.664 (p<0.001), while emotion2vec Valence shows rho=+0.097 (p=0.499). EMO-DB evaluation reveals acted speech, cultural bias, and category incompatibility.

Ablation

Not explicitly reported; the study focuses on correlation comparisons and EMO-DB quality evaluation rather than ablation.

Limitations

['Single-speaker case study (Banaszak) limits generalizability to other politicians or languages.', 'Post-hoc Russell Circumplex projection from emotion2vec relies on unverified assumptions about weight transferability.', 'TRUST-Pathos scores themselves are LLM-based, so correlation with Gemini may reflect shared model biases.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Highlights the need for ecologically valid emotion recognition benchmarks for political speech analysis.
  2. Suggests that LLM-based multimodal approaches may be more suitable than acoustic-only models for capturing complex political emotions.
  3. Provides a methodology for comparing acoustic and LLM-based emotion analysis in a controlled setting.

6) Next steps

1) 阅读理由

本文通过德国联邦议院演讲案例，证明基于LLM的多模态分析比纯声学情感识别更能有效捕捉政治相关的悲情维度。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

上下文：TRUST框架从逻辑、信誉和悲情三个维度评估政治演讲。悲情需要识别词汇内容、韵律和修辞中的情感状态，而声学SER模型在表演式基准上虽有进展，但在自然政治演讲中的有效性尚未探索。相关工作：SER基准（如EMO-DB、IEMOCAP）依赖固定句子的表演式语音；LLM（如Gemini）在开放式音频和文本情感理解中表现良好；TRUST管道将悲情操作化为情感语言对社会的影响（-2到+2量表）。差距：声学SER模型通常在表演式语料库上评估，可能无法泛化到自然政治演讲。具体问题是声学情感特征能否作为政治相关悲情维度的有效代理。提案：作者比较了emotion2vec声学特征、Gemini多模态分析和TRUST-Pathos评分在51个联邦议院演讲片段上的表现，并引入事后Russell Circumplex投影从离散SER类别概率推导连续唤醒度/效价，同时用Gemini作为开放式标注器评估EMO-DB以揭示结构局限。

3) 实验

在41个有效Banaszak演讲片段上，Gemini效价与TRUST-Pathos的相关系数为rho=+0.664（p<0.001），而emotion2vec效价的相关系数为rho=+0.097（p=0.499）。EMO-DB评估揭示了表演式语音、文化偏见和类别不兼容问题。

消融实验

未明确报告消融实验；研究主要关注相关性比较和EMO-DB质量评估。

局限性

单演讲者案例研究（Banaszak）限制了向其他政治家或语言的泛化性。从emotion2vec进行事后Russell Circumplex投影依赖关于权重可转移性的未经验证假设。TRUST-Pathos评分本身基于LLM，因此与Gemini的相关性可能反映共享模型偏差。

4) 重要性

  1. 强调需要为政治演讲分析建立生态效度高的情感识别基准。
  2. 表明基于LLM的多模态方法可能比纯声学模型更适合捕捉复杂政治情感。
  3. 提供了在受控环境中比较声学和LLM情感分析的方法论。
