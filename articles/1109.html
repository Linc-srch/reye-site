1) Why-read

STT-Arena introduces a benchmark for evaluating LLMs' adaptive replanning under spatio-temporal dynamics, revealing that even the best proprietary models achieve under 40% accuracy.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLM-based agents are increasingly deployed in real-world applications requiring multi-step tool use. Existing benchmarks focus on static environments or temporal change detection, but neglect the need for replanning when mid-task disruptions invalidate prior decisions.

R — Related work

  • GAIA-2, Real-Time Reasoning Gym, and Timely Machine emphasize real-time environmental change and responsiveness.
  • API-Bank, ToolBench, and StableToolBench evaluate static tool use without spatio-temporal dynamics.
  • τ-Bench and τ²-Bench introduce temporal elements but lack spatial and spatio-temporal dimensions.

G — Gap

Current dynamic benchmarks primarily measure timely detection of temporal changes, not the ability to abandon a failed plan and reconstruct an alternative strategy under spatio-temporal dynamics. The complementary challenge of adaptive replanning when both time and space conditions shift remains largely unexplored.

P — Proposal

STT-Arena provides 227 interactive tasks with nine spatio-temporal conflict types and four solvability levels, each grounded in executable environments with injected triggers. The authors also propose an iterative trajectory refinement technique combined with online RL to produce STT-Agent-4B, which matches frontier LLM performance.

3) Figures

4) Experiments

Main results

Claude-4.6-Opus achieves less than 40% overall accuracy on STT-Arena. STT-Agent-4B achieves 27.17% accuracy, matching GLM-5.1.

Ablation

The iterative trajectory refinement technique eliminates three recurring error modes (Stale-State Execution, Misdiagnosis of Dynamic Triggers, Missing Post-Adaptation Verification) from training data, enabling STT-Agent-4B to outperform frontier LLMs.

Limitations

['The benchmark contains only 227 tasks, which may limit statistical power and coverage of real-world scenarios.', 'The spatio-temporal triggers are injected and may not fully capture the complexity of natural dynamics.', 'The passive user simulator provides no additional information beyond the initial query, potentially limiting ecological validity.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Highlights the critical gap in LLM agent evaluation: adaptive replanning under spatio-temporal dynamics is fundamentally harder than temporal change detection.
  2. Provides a taxonomy of spatio-temporal conflict types that can guide future benchmark design and model training.
  3. Demonstrates that iterative trajectory refinement combined with online RL can effectively address identified failure modes, offering a practical path to improve agent robustness.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Incorporate iterative trajectory refinement into your training pipeline to eliminate Stale-State Execution and Misdiagnosis of Dynamic Triggers.
