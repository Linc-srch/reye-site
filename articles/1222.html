LANG: Reinforcement Learning for Multilingual Reasoning with Language-Adaptive Hint Guidance

LANG：面向多语言推理的语言自适应提示引导强化学习框架

Yuchun Fan, Bei Li, Peiguang Li, Yilin Wang, Yongyu Mu, Jian Yang, Xin Chen, Rongxiang Weng, Jingang Wang, Xunliang Cai, Jingbo Zhu, Tong Xiao

Summary

LANG proposes a language-adaptive hint-guided RL framework that simultaneously improves multilingual reasoning accuracy and language consistency by gradually withdrawing scaffolding tailored to each language's difficulty.

摘要

本文提出LANG框架，通过语言条件化提示引导强化学习探索，并采用渐进式提示衰减调度和语言自适应开关，有效缓解了多语言推理中的奖励稀疏和训练-推理不一致问题。在MMATH和PolyMath基准上，LANG相比基线GRPO分别提升24.1%和18.7%的准确率，同时保持了语言一致性。

Deep Note — lang

Key Takeaways - LANG uses language-conditioned hints to bootstrap RL exploration for multilingual reasoning, addressing reward sparsity. - A progressive hint-decay schedule and language-adaptive switch prevent over-reliance on hints and tailor training to language difficulty. - LANG improves accuracy over vanilla GRPO by 24.1% on MMATH and 18.7% on PolyMath while maintaining language consistency. - The framework generalizes beyond math to other multilingual tasks and promotes consistent language alignment across model layers.

1) Why-read

LANG proposes a language-adaptive hint-guided RL framework that simultaneously improves multilingual reasoning accuracy and language consistency by gradually withdrawing scaffolding tailored to each language's difficulty.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) has boosted multi-step reasoning in LLMs, but remains English-centric. Multilingual reasoning faces a trade-off: enforcing input-language consistency degrades accuracy, while allowing language drift toward English harms native-user interpretability. Existing methods use prompt constraints or language-consistency rewards, but suffer from reward sparsity and limited exploration in non-English settings.

R — Related work

  • Multilingual mathematical reasoning: models show uneven performance across languages and often drift to English (Fan et al., 2025a; Zhang et al., 2025b; Wang et al., 2025b; Luo et al., 2025).
  • Language-consistency approaches: prompt constraints (Qi et al., 2025; Luo et al., 2025) and language-consistency rewards in RL (Park et al., 2025; Yang et al., 2025b; mGRPO, 2025; Hwang et al., 2025).
  • Hint-guided RL: using partial solutions as in-context guidance to mitigate reward sparsity (Zhang et al., 2025a; Wang et al., 2025a; Liu et al., 2025; QUESTA Li et al., 2025).

G — Gap

Existing hint-guided RL methods for multilingual reasoning suffer from a severe training-inference discrepancy: models rely on hints during training but must reason autonomously at test time, leading to performance drops and repetitive generation. Furthermore, language difficulty varies, but prior work does not adapt hint scheduling per language, causing suboptimal learning for both easy and hard languages.

P — Proposal

LANG introduces language-conditioned hints as scaffolding for RL exploration in non-English reasoning, combined with a progressive hint-decay schedule that gradually reduces hint exposure over training. A language-adaptive switch sets language-specific horizons for turning off hints, tailoring the learning process to each language's difficulty and preventing dependency while improving autonomous reasoning.

4) Experiments

Main results

On MMATH, LANG improves accuracy over vanilla GRPO by 24.1%; on PolyMath, by 18.7%. Experiments use Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and another LLM across two multilingual math benchmarks. LANG also generalizes to non-mathematical multilingual tasks, showing consistent language alignment across layers.

Ablation

Ablation studies (implied by pilot study) show that naively applying fixed hints (QUESTA) leads to higher training reward but worse test accuracy, increased response length dominated by repetition, and higher policy entropy that does not translate to better performance. LANG's progressive decay and language-adaptive switch are key to closing the training-inference gap.

Limitations

['The method relies on the availability of multilingual reasoning traces for hint construction, which may be costly for low-resource languages.', 'The language-adaptive switch requires tuning of language-specific thresholds, which may not generalize to unseen languages without additional calibration.', 'Evaluation is primarily on mathematical reasoning; generalization to other domains (e.g., commonsense or scientific reasoning) is shown but may be limited.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Addresses a critical bottleneck in deploying reasoning LLMs for non-English users, balancing accuracy and language consistency.
  2. Provides a principled way to adapt RL training dynamics to language difficulty, which could inspire similar adaptive strategies for other multimodal or domain-specific tasks.
  3. Highlights the training-inference discrepancy in hint-guided RL, a general issue that extends beyond multilingual reasoning.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Apply LANG to more low-resource languages and evaluate on diverse reasoning benchmarks (e.g., multilingual MMLU, GSM8K variants).
  • [ ] Explore automatic hint generation using weaker models or retrieval to reduce reliance on curated traces.
  • [ ] Investigate combining LANG with other RL improvements (e.g., reward shaping, curriculum learning) for further gains.
  • [ ] Analyze the effect of hint decay schedules on model internal representations and language alignment across layers.

深度笔记

LANG：面向多语言推理的语言自适应提示引导强化学习框架

主要结论 - LANG利用语言条件化提示作为脚手架，引导强化学习在多语言推理中的探索，解决了奖励稀疏问题。 - 渐进式提示衰减调度和语言自适应开关防止模型过度依赖提示，并根据语言难度定制训练过程。 - 在MMATH和PolyMath上，LANG相比vanilla GRPO分别提升24.1%和18.7%的准确率，同时保持语言一致性。 - 该框架可泛化到数学以外的多语言任务，并在模型各层促进一致的语言对齐。

1) 阅读理由

LANG提出了一种语言自适应的提示引导强化学习框架，通过逐步撤回针对每种语言难度定制的脚手架，同时提升了多语言推理的准确性和语言一致性。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

上下文：基于可验证奖励的强化学习（RLVR）提升了LLM的多步推理能力，但主要集中于英语。多语言推理面临一个权衡：强制输入语言一致性会降低准确率，而允许向英语漂移则损害母语用户的可解释性。现有方法使用提示约束或语言一致性奖励，但在非英语环境中面临奖励稀疏和探索受限的问题。相关工作：多语言数学推理模型在不同语言上表现不均且常向英语漂移；语言一致性方法包括提示约束和强化学习中的语言一致性奖励；提示引导强化学习使用部分解决方案作为上下文指导来缓解奖励稀疏。差距：现有的提示引导强化学习方法存在严重的训练-推理不一致：模型在训练时依赖提示，但在测试时必须自主推理，导致性能下降和重复生成。此外，语言难度各异，但先前工作未根据语言调整提示调度，导致对简单和困难语言的学习均不理想。提案：LANG引入语言条件化提示作为非英语推理中强化学习探索的脚手架，结合渐进式提示衰减调度逐步减少训练中的提示暴露。语言自适应开关为每种语言设置关闭提示的特定时间点，使学习过程适应语言难度，防止依赖并提升自主推理能力。

3) 实验

在MMATH上，LANG相比vanilla GRPO准确率提升24.1%；在PolyMath上提升18.7%。实验使用Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct和另一个大语言模型，在两个多语言数学基准上进行。LANG还能泛化到非数学的多语言任务，在各层显示出一致的语言对齐。

消融实验

消融研究表明，简单应用固定提示（QUESTA）会导致训练奖励更高但测试准确率更差，响应长度增加且以重复为主，策略熵更高但未转化为更好的性能。LANG的渐进式衰减和语言自适应开关是缩小训练-推理差距的关键。

局限性

['该方法依赖多语言推理轨迹来构建提示，对于低资源语言可能成本高昂。', '语言自适应开关需要调整语言特定的阈值，未经额外校准可能无法泛化到未见语言。', '评估主要基于数学推理；对其他领域（如常识或科学推理）的泛化虽有展示但可能有限。']

4) 重要性

  1. 解决了为非英语用户部署推理大语言模型的关键瓶颈，平衡了准确性和语言一致性。
  2. 提供了一种将强化学习训练动态适应语言难度的原则性方法，可启发其他多模态或领域特定任务的类似自适应策略。
  3. 揭示了提示引导强化学习中的训练-推理不一致问题，这是一个超越多语言推理的普遍问题。

5) 下一步

  • [ ] 将LANG应用于更多低资源语言，并在多样化推理基准（如多语言MMLU、GSM8K变体）上评估。
  • [ ] 探索使用较弱模型或检索自动生成提示，以减少对精心策划轨迹的依赖。
  • [ ] 研究将LANG与其他强化学习改进（如奖励塑形、课程学习）结合以进一步提升性能。
