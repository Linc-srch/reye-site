CERT-In Recommends 12-Hour Patching for Internet-Facing Flaws Amid AI-Assisted Attacks

CERT-In Recommends 12-Hour Patching for Internet-Facing Flaws Amid AI-Assisted Attacks

rlhf supply-chain-attack zero-trust
Summary

The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) has issued new guidelines requiring organizations to patch critical vulnerabilities in internet-exposed systems within 12 hours where feasible, citing the accelerating threat of AI-assisted cyberattacks. The 38-page blueprint warns that AI tools and large language models enable adversaries to automate vulnerability discovery, exploitation, and malware generation, compressing attack timelines. It recommends a Zero Trust approach, defense-in-depth, continuous vulnerability management, and secure-by-design principles for AI workflows. The guidelines also address risks to AI systems themselves, including prompt injections and model theft, urging organizations to adopt layered, risk-based controls to reduce exposure and maintain operational resilience.

摘要

印度CERT-In发布新指南，要求组织在可行情况下12小时内修补面向互联网系统的关键漏洞，以应对AI辅助网络攻击的加速威胁。该38页蓝图警告，AI工具和LLM使攻击者能够自动化漏洞发现、利用和恶意软件生成，压缩攻击时间线。指南建议采用Zero Trust、纵深防御、持续漏洞管理及AI工作流的secure-by-design原则，并强调AI系统自身面临prompt注入、模型窃取等风险，敦促组织通过分层、基于风险的控制减少暴露并保持运营韧性。
