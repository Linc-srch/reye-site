1) Why-read

Proposes value-based personas derived from survey responses and a calibration procedure to improve LLM-based cross-cultural survey simulation, reducing prediction error especially for underrepresented populations.

2) CRGP

C — Context

LLMs are increasingly used to simulate human survey responses, but existing methods rely on sociodemographic or country-based prompts that fail to capture cultural variation and produce overconfident, biased outputs. Prior work shows that even with country-specific prompting, LLM responses remain anchored to Western cultural priors and lack within-population diversity.

R — Related work

  • Argyle et al. (2023) and Qi et al. (2025) use demographic personas to steer LLM responses but only partially recover country-specific value orientations.
  • Miranda and Balbi (2025) use value-conditioned prompting with LLM-selected variables but focus on U.S. data and rely on uncalibrated temperature for diversity.
  • Tao et al. (2024) and Kharchenko et al. (2024) show that country-specific prompting reduces but does not eliminate cultural bias in LLMs.
  • Suh et al. (2025) use finetuning on survey data but generalize poorly to unseen questions and require model weight access.

G — Gap

Existing persona-based methods use indirect proxies (demographics, country labels) that fail to capture underlying value systems, leading to biased and under-diverse simulations. No prior work systematically constructs personas from individual-level value profiles sampled from target populations and calibrates response diversity while preserving aggregate estimates.

P — Proposal

The authors construct value-based personas by converting individual-level survey responses on Inglehart-Welzel cultural dimensions into natural language descriptors, then sample these personas from target populations to generate LLM responses. A mean-preserving calibration procedure corrects under-dispersion of LLM outputs, improving alignment with human response distributions without sacrificing aggregate accuracy.

4) Experiments

Main results

Using Gemma-4-31B, the approach reduces prediction error across countries, with largest improvements for underrepresented populations (e.g., error reduction of X% compared to country-prompting baselines). The calibrated response distributions closely match human diversity, narrowing the performance gap between WEIRD and non-WEIRD countries.

Ablation

Calibration preserves aggregate opinion estimates while increasing response diversity; without calibration, LLM responses are much less diverse than human data (e.g., standard deviation of responses increases from Y to Z after calibration, matching human levels).

Limitations

['Evaluation is limited to one LLM (Gemma-4-31B) and one survey framework (WVS Inglehart-Welzel dimensions).', 'The method requires access to individual-level survey data for value profile sampling, which may not be available for all target populations.', 'Calibration procedure may not generalize to other types of survey questions or value dimensions beyond the two studied.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses cultural bias in LLM simulation, relevant to your work on fair and representative AI systems.
  2. Provides a principled method to ground LLM personas in empirical value distributions rather than stereotypes.
  3. Calibration technique for improving response diversity is applicable to other LLM simulation tasks beyond surveys.
  4. Demonstrates a path to reduce performance gaps for underrepresented populations in LLM-based social science research.

6) Next steps

  • [ ] Test the method on other LLMs (e.g., GPT-4, Llama-3) and survey frameworks (e.g., European Social Survey).
  • [ ] Explore automatic extraction of value dimensions from survey data without relying on pre-validated frameworks.
  • [ ] Apply calibration to other domains where LLM output diversity is critical, such as opinion generation or debate simulation.
  • [ ] Investigate whether value-based personas can be combined with demographic attributes for even finer-grained simulation.
