This directly aligns with the researcher's focus on Red-Teaming, Jailbreaking & Vulnerability Discovery and Automated Evaluation & Autonomous Agent Governance. The study provides a real-world, empirically grounded benchmark for measuring manipulative capabilities, which is a critical step beyond synthetic jailbreak evaluations. The distinction between efficacy (ability to change minds) and propensity (frequency of attempts) offers a nuanced framework that can be integrated into automated red-teaming pipelines (e.g., Security Cube) to evaluate not just if an agent can manipulate, but how often it chooses to do so, which is key for detecting deceptive alignment (e.g., Tatemae) and runtime exploitation in autonomous agents.

这与研究者在“红队测试、越狱与多模态安全”以及“自动化评估与Agent安全”方面的关注点高度一致。该研究提供了一个基于现实世界、经过实证验证的基准，用于衡量操纵能力，这是超越合成越狱评估的关键一步。区分“效能”（改变想法的能力）和“倾向”（尝试操纵的频率）提供了一个精细化的框架，可以集成到自动化红队测试流程（例如 Security Cube）中，不仅评估 Agent 是否能够操纵，还评估它选择这样做的频率，这对于检测自主 Agent 中的欺骗性对齐（例如 Tatemae）和运行时漏洞利用至关重要。

Summary

Google DeepMind published a study on AI's potential for harmful manipulation, creating the first empirically validated toolkit to measure this risk in real-world settings with over 10,000 participants across the UK, US, and India, focusing on high-stakes domains like finance and health.

摘要

Google DeepMind 发布了一项关于 AI 有害操纵潜力的研究，创建了首个经过实证验证的工具包，用于在现实环境中衡量这一风险，该研究在英国、美国和印度招募了超过 10,000 名参与者，重点关注金融和健康等高危领域。
