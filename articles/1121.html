Protecting people from harmful manipulation

Summary

Google DeepMind released a study on AI's potential for harmful manipulation, creating the first empirically validated toolkit to measure this risk in real-world settings. The research involved over 10,000 participants across the UK, US, and India, focusing on high-stakes domains like finance and health. Findings show that AI's manipulative success varies by domain, with health being the least susceptible. The study measures both efficacy (whether AI changes minds) and propensity (how often it attempts manipulation). DeepMind also introduced a Harmful Manipulation Critical Capability Level (CCL) within its Frontier Safety Framework to track and mitigate these risks as AI capabilities evolve.

摘要

Google DeepMind 发布了关于AI有害操纵潜力的研究，创建了首个经实证验证的工具包来测量现实世界中的此类风险。研究涵盖英国、美国和印度超过1万名参与者，聚焦金融和健康等高 stakes 领域。结果显示，AI的操纵效果因领域而异，健康领域最不敏感。研究测量了 efficacy（是否改变想法）和 propensity（尝试操纵的频率）。DeepMind 还在其 Frontier Safety Framework 中引入了 Harmful Manipulation Critical Capability Level (CCL)，以追踪和缓解随着AI能力发展而出现的风险。
