Kimwolf DDoS Botnet Operator Arrested in Canada Over DDoS-for-Hire Attacks

Kimwolf DDoS Botnet Operator Arrested in Canada Over DDoS-for-Hire Attacks

Summary

Canadian national Jacob Butler (aka Dort) was arrested for operating the Kimwolf DDoS botnet, a variant of AISURU that infected Android devices via exposed Android Debug Bridge (ADB) services. The botnet enslaved IoT devices like digital photo frames and webcams to launch distributed denial-of-service attacks, including against U.S. Department of Defense networks. Kimwolf issued over 25,000 attack commands and was part of record-setting DDoS events reaching 31.4 Tbps. The arrest follows a joint international takedown of the botnet's command-and-control infrastructure by the U.S., Canada, and Germany. Butler faces up to 10 years in prison for aiding and abetting computer intrusion.

摘要

加拿大男子Jacob Butler（化名Dort）因运营Kimwolf DDoS僵尸网络被捕。Kimwolf是AISURU的变种，通过暴露的Android Debug Bridge (ADB)服务感染Android设备，将数字相框、网络摄像头等IoT设备沦为肉鸡，用于发起分布式拒绝服务（DDoS）攻击，目标包括美国国防部网络。该僵尸网络发布了超过25,000条攻击指令，并参与了峰值达31.4 Tbps的创纪录攻击。此次逮捕是美国、加拿大和德国联合执法行动的一部分。Butler被控协助和教唆计算机入侵，最高面临10年监禁。
