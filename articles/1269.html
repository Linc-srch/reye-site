Content Delivery Exploit Opens Websites to Brand Hijacking

Content Delivery Exploit Opens Websites to Brand Hijacking

content-management supply-chain-attack zero-trust Network Security Vulnerabilities & Exploits
Summary

Researchers have identified a new domain-fronting exploit called Underminr that allows attackers to hijack the brand reputation of legitimate websites by manipulating DNS and CDN interactions. Unlike earlier domain fronting, Underminr bypasses existing mitigations by exploiting the lack of cross-referencing between DNS and CDN systems, enabling malicious traffic to be routed through trusted domains. ADAMnetworks found that 42% of global websites are vulnerable, with US sites at 51% risk. The attack can be used for scams, C2 operations, and data exfiltration while evading detection. Mitigation requires CDNs to adopt reputation-based domain grouping, as exemplified by Fastly.

摘要

研究人员发现了一种名为Underminr的新型域名前端攻击，攻击者通过操纵DNS和CDN交互，利用合法网站的品牌声誉进行恶意活动。与早期的domain fronting不同，Underminr绕过了现有缓解措施，利用DNS和CDN系统之间缺乏交叉验证的漏洞，将恶意流量通过受信任域名路由。ADAMnetworks发现全球42%的网站易受攻击，美国网站风险高达51%。攻击可用于诈骗、C2操作和数据窃取，同时规避检测。缓解措施要求CDN采用基于声誉的域名分组，如Fastly的做法。
