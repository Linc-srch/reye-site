Rewarding Beliefs, Not Actions: Consistency-Guided Credit Assignment for Long-Horizon Agents

奖励信念而非行动：面向长周期智能体的一致性引导信用分配方法

Wenjie Tang, Minne Li, Sijie Huang, Liquan Xiao, Yuan Zhou

Summary

ReBel introduces a belief-consistency credit assignment method that turns sparse terminal rewards into dense self-supervised signals for long-horizon LLM agents under partial observability, achieving significant gains over episode-level and step-level baselines.

摘要

本文提出ReBel方法，通过信念一致性监督将稀疏的终端奖励转化为密集的自监督信号，有效解决了部分可观测环境下长周期LLM智能体的信用分配难题。在ALFWorld和WebShop基准上，ReBel相比GRPO将任务成功率提升高达20.4个百分点，并实现了2.1倍的样本效率提升。

2) CRGP

C — Context

Reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards (RLVR) is a promising paradigm for improving LLM agents on long-horizon interactive tasks. However, in partially observable environments, incomplete observations cause agent beliefs to drift over time, and delayed rewards obscure the causal impact of intermediate decisions, exacerbating temporal credit assignment challenges.

R — Related work

  • RLHF methods like InstructGPT align model outputs with human intent via preference-based optimization.
  • Trajectory-level RL methods such as RAP, Agent Q, ArCHer, GRPO, and RLOO optimize policies in interactive environments.
  • Process supervision methods like Math-Shepherd, PRIME, and Watch Every Step provide automated step-level rewards.
  • Belief-centric approaches include retrieval augmentation, symbolic augmentation, and intrinsic motivation mechanisms like curiosity and random network distillation.

G — Gap

Existing RLVR methods rely on terminal rewards or assume near-complete observability, making them vulnerable to severe credit assignment challenges in partially observable long-horizon tasks. Step-wise supervision or external verifiers are costly and often unavailable, and observation-conditioned grouping fails to reflect the agent's latent state, leading to degraded grouping quality and unstable advantage normalization.

P — Proposal

ReBel explicitly models structured belief states to summarize interaction history and guide policy learning. It introduces belief-consistency supervision, converting discrepancies between predicted beliefs and observed feedback into dense self-supervised signals, and employs belief-aware grouping to compare trajectories under similar belief states for more robust advantage estimates.

4) Experiments

Main results

On ALFWorld, ReBel achieves 93.2 ± 4.1% success rate, improving over GRPO by 20.4 percentage points and over GiGPO w/o std by 7.1 percentage points. On WebShop, ReBel achieves 75.1 ± 2.7% success rate, improving over GRPO by 20.4 percentage points and over GiGPO w/o std by 7.7 percentage points. ReBel reaches GRPO's final performance in ~35 training iterations, yielding a 2.1x sample-efficiency gain.

Ablation

Belief-consistency supervision and belief-aware grouping both contribute to performance; removing either component leads to degradation, with belief-aware grouping providing more robust advantage estimation and lower variance.

Limitations

['ReBel requires defining a structured belief representation for each task, which may not be straightforward for all domains.', 'The method assumes that belief states can be explicitly predicted and compared, which may be challenging in highly stochastic environments.', 'Evaluation is limited to two benchmarks (ALFWorld and WebShop); generalization to other long-horizon tasks is not yet demonstrated.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Provides a principled way to address belief drift in partially observable environments, a key challenge for deploying LLM agents in real-world interactive tasks.
  2. Demonstrates that dense self-supervised signals from belief consistency can replace expensive step-wise annotations, making RLVR more scalable.
  3. The belief-aware grouping technique offers a general mechanism for reducing variance in advantage estimation under partial observability.

深度笔记

奖励信念而非行动：面向长周期智能体的一致性引导信用分配方法

主要结论 - ReBel利用信念一致性监督，将预测信念与观测反馈之间的不匹配转化为密集的自监督信号，无需逐步标注。 - 信念感知分组通过比较相似信念状态下的轨迹，获得更稳健、方差更低的优势估计。 - 在ALFWorld和WebShop上，ReBel相比GRPO将任务成功率提升高达20.4个百分点，样本效率提升2.1倍。 - 该方法显式建模结构化信念状态，以总结交互历史并指导部分可观测环境中的策略学习。

1) 阅读理由

ReBel提出了一种信念一致性信用分配方法，将稀疏的终端奖励转化为密集的自监督信号，在部分可观测环境下显著优于基于回合级和步骤级的基线方法。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：基于可验证奖励的强化学习（RLVR）是提升LLM智能体在长周期交互任务中表现的有前景范式。然而，在部分可观测环境中，不完整观测导致智能体信念随时间漂移，延迟奖励掩盖了中间决策的因果影响，加剧了时间信用分配挑战。

相关工作：RLHF方法（如InstructGPT）通过偏好优化对齐模型输出与人类意图；轨迹级RL方法（如RAP、Agent Q、ArCHer、GRPO、RLOO）在交互环境中优化策略；过程监督方法（如Math-Shepherd、PRIME、Watch Every Step）提供自动化的步骤级奖励；信念中心方法包括检索增强、符号增强以及好奇心、随机网络蒸馏等内在动机机制。

差距：现有RLVR方法依赖终端奖励或假设近乎完全可观测性，在部分可观测的长周期任务中面临严重的信用分配挑战。步骤级监督或外部验证器成本高昂且通常不可用，而基于观测的分组无法反映智能体的潜在状态，导致分组质量下降和优势归一化不稳定。

提案：ReBel显式建模结构化信念状态以总结交互历史并指导策略学习。它引入信念一致性监督，将预测信念与观测反馈之间的差异转化为密集的自监督信号，并采用信念感知分组在相似信念状态下比较轨迹，以获得更稳健的优势估计。

3) 实验

在ALFWorld上，ReBel达到93.2 ± 4.1%的成功率，相比GRPO提升20.4个百分点，相比无标准差的GiGPO提升7.1个百分点。在WebShop上，ReBel达到75.1 ± 2.7%的成功率，相比GRPO提升20.4个百分点，相比无标准差的GiGPO提升7.7个百分点。ReBel在约35次训练迭代中达到GRPO的最终性能，实现了2.1倍的样本效率提升。

消融实验

信念一致性监督和信念感知分组均对性能有贡献；移除任一组件都会导致性能下降，其中信念感知分组提供了更稳健的优势估计和更低的方差。

局限性

['ReBel需要为每个任务定义结构化信念表示，这在某些领域可能并不直接。', '该方法假设信念状态可以被显式预测和比较，在高度随机的环境中可能具有挑战性。', '评估仅限于两个基准（ALFWorld和WebShop），尚未证明对其他长周期任务的泛化能力。']

4) 重要性

  1. 为解决部分可观测环境中的信念漂移问题提供了原则性方法，这是将LLM智能体部署到真实世界交互任务的关键挑战。
  2. 证明了来自信念一致性的密集自监督信号可以替代昂贵的逐步标注，使RLVR更具可扩展性。
  3. 信念感知分组技术提供了一种在部分可观测性下降低优势估计方差的通用机制。
