Microsoft Takes Down Malware-Signing Service Behind Ransomware Attacks

Microsoft Takes Down Malware-Signing Service Behind Ransomware Attacks

supply-chain-attack Malware & Threats Supply Chain Security
Summary

Microsoft disrupted a malware-signing-as-a-service (MSaaS) operation, dubbed Fox Tempest, which abused the company's Artifact Signing system to generate fraudulent code-signing certificates for signing malware. The service enabled ransomware attacks, including Rhysida, and distributed malware like Oyster, Lumma Stealer, and Vidar. Fox Tempest used stolen identities from the US and Canada to obtain legitimate certificates and offered the service for $5,000–$9,000. The takedown, OpFauxSign, seized the website, virtual machines, and hosting infrastructure. The operation targeted healthcare, education, government, and financial sectors globally. Microsoft emphasized that such abuse undermines trust in software legitimacy and security controls.

摘要

微软捣毁了一个名为Fox Tempest的恶意签名服务平台，该平台滥用微软的Artifact Signing系统生成伪造的代码签名证书，为勒索软件（如Rhysida）及其他恶意软件签名。该服务使用美国与加拿大的被盗身份获取合法证书，收费5000-9000美元，并通过Cloudzy虚拟机分发。行动代号OpFauxSign，查封了网站和基础设施。此举突显了代码签名信任机制被武器化的风险。
