Text-to-SPARQL Generation with Reinforcement Learning: A GRPO-based Approach on DBLP

Deep Note — text2sparql-grpo

Jann Pfeifer, Debayan Banerjee, Ricardo Usbeck · 2026-05-20 · arxiv

Summary

This paper demonstrates that reinforcement learning with outcome-based rewards can train a small 1.7B language model to generate SPARQL queries zero-shot, achieving competitive results without gold query supervision.

摘要

本文展示了如何通过基于结果奖励的强化学习（GRPO）训练一个1.7B的小语言模型，在无需金标准查询标注的情况下，实现零样本Text-to-SPARQL生成，并在DBLP-QuAD基准上取得38.4%的准确率。与需要金标准查询的监督微调（DoRA）相比，该方法虽然准确率略低（47.2% vs 38.4%），但在标注稀缺的实际场景中更具可行性。

Deep Note

Deep Note — text2sparql-grpo

Key Takeaways - GRPO training on a 1.7B model improves zero-shot Text-to-SPARQL accuracy from 0.0% to 38.4% on DBLP-QuAD. - Execution-based rewards account for most of the gain; additional gold-query shaping provides limited benefit. - Supervised DoRA finetuning achieves higher overall accuracy (47.2%) but requires gold queries unavailable in many settings. - Outcome-based RL is a viable alternative when gold query annotations are absent.

1) Why-read

This paper demonstrates that reinforcement learning with outcome-based rewards can train a small 1.7B language model to generate SPARQL queries zero-shot, achieving competitive results without gold query supervision.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Knowledge graph question answering (KGQA) typically relies on large language models or full supervision with gold SPARQL queries. Recent work has applied RL to Text-to-SQL, but its use for single-step Text-to-SPARQL generation with small models remains underexplored. The DBLP-QuAD benchmark provides a scholarly KGQA testbed with 10,000 questions.

R — Related work

  • Supervised encoder-decoder models (T5, BART) for semantic parsing on LC-QuAD and QALD.
  • LLM-based few-shot and agentic pipelines for KGQA (e.g., D'Abranno et al., 2025; Strappazon et al., 2025).
  • RL for Text-to-SQL using execution-based rewards (Chen et al., 2025a; Pourreza et al., 2025).
  • GRPO applied to iterative SPARQL refinement over Wikidata (Vossebeld and Wang, 2025).
  • Small model fine-tuning for structured query generation (Karki et al., 2025; Nguyen et al., 2025).

G — Gap

Existing approaches for Text-to-SPARQL either require large models, gold query annotations, or complex agentic pipelines. There is limited evidence on whether a small open-weight model can be trained via outcome-driven RL for single-step scholarly Text-to-SPARQL, and how such training compares to supervised fine-tuning at the same scale.

P — Proposal

The authors apply Group-Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) to the Qwen3-1.7B model on DBLP-QuAD, using prompts with symbolic hints (entity/relation URIs and schema info). The reward combines execution feedback (F1 score), structural coverage, format compliance, and length penalties. They compare two GRPO variants (with and without gold-query shaping) against zero-shot and supervised DoRA baselines.

3) Figures

4) Experiments

Main results

GRPO improves answer-level accuracy from 0.0% (zero-shot) to 38.4% on DBLP-QuAD. Supervised DoRA achieves 47.2%. Execution accuracy reaches 42.1% for GRPO vs 51.8% for DoRA. On held-out templates, GRPO generalizes competitively (e.g., 34.7% vs 41.2% for DoRA).

Ablation

Removing execution-based reward (Rexec) drops accuracy from 38.4% to 12.1%, while removing structural coverage (Rstruct) reduces it to 31.5%. Gold-query shaping adds only +1.2% over execution-only rewards.

Limitations

['Assumes perfect entity and relation linking, removing a major source of end-to-end errors.', 'Single-step generation; does not explore iterative refinement or multi-turn interaction.', 'Limited to the DBLP scholarly domain; generalizability to other KGs is not tested.']

5) Why it matters

  1. Shows that small models (1.7B) can be trained for structured query generation without gold queries, reducing dependency on large LLMs.
  2. Provides a practical pipeline for domain-specific KGQA where gold annotations are scarce.
  3. Highlights the effectiveness of execution feedback as a reward signal, relevant for your work on RL for code generation.

Deep Note — text2sparql-grpo

主要结论 - GRPO训练使1.7B模型在DBLP-QuAD上的零样本Text-to-SPARQL准确率从0.0%提升至38.4%。 - 执行反馈（Execution-based reward）是性能提升的主要来源，而金标准查询形状奖励（gold-query shaping）带来的增益有限（仅+1.2%）。 - 监督DoRA微调准确率更高（47.2%），但需要金标准查询，这在许多实际场景中不可得。 - 基于结果的强化学习是金标准查询缺失时的一种可行替代方案。

1) 阅读理由

本文的关键主张是：基于结果奖励的强化学习可以训练小模型生成SPARQL查询，关键观察是执行反馈比金标准查询形状奖励更有效。

2) CRGP（背景 / 相关工作 / 差距 / 方案）

背景：知识图谱问答（KGQA）通常依赖大语言模型或带有金标准SPARQL查询的完全监督。最近强化学习被应用于Text-to-SQL，但在单步Text-to-SPARQL生成中结合小模型的研究仍不充分。DBLP-QuAD基准提供了包含10,000个问题的学术KGQA测试平台。

相关工作：监督式编码器-解码器模型（T5, BART）用于LC-QuAD和QALD的语义解析；基于LLM的少样本和智能体流水线用于KGQA（如D'Abranno等人，2025；Strappazon等人，2025）；基于执行奖励的强化学习用于Text-to-SQL（Chen等人，2025a；Pourreza等人，2025）；GRPO用于Wikidata上的迭代SPARQL优化（Vossebeld和Wang，2025）；小模型微调用于结构化查询生成（Karki等人，2025；Nguyen等人，2025）。

差距：现有Text-to-SPARQL方法要么需要大模型、金标准查询标注，要么需要复杂的智能体流水线。目前缺乏证据表明小规模开放权重模型能否通过基于结果的强化学习进行单步学术Text-to-SPARQL训练，以及这种训练与同规模监督微调的对比。

提案：作者将组相对策略优化（GRPO）应用于Qwen3-1.7B模型，在DBLP-QuAD上使用包含符号提示（实体/关系URI和模式信息）的提示。奖励结合了执行反馈（F1分数）、结构覆盖、格式合规和长度惩罚。他们比较了两种GRPO变体（有无金标准查询形状奖励）与零样本和监督DoRA基线。

3) 实验

主要结果：GRPO将答案级准确率从0.0%（零样本）提升至38.4%（DBLP-QuAD）。监督DoRA达到47.2%。执行准确率方面，GRPO为42.1%，DoRA为51.8%。在保留模板上，GRPO泛化能力具有竞争力（例如34.7% vs DoRA的41.2%）。

消融实验

消融实验：移除执行奖励（Rexec）使准确率从38.4%降至12.1%；移除结构覆盖奖励（Rstruct）使准确率降至31.5%。金标准查询形状奖励仅比纯执行奖励提升+1.2%。

局限性

['假设实体和关系链接完美，忽略了端到端错误的主要来源。', '仅支持单步生成，未探索迭代优化或多轮交互。', '局限于DBLP学术领域，未测试对其他知识图谱的泛化能力。']

4) 重要性

  1. 表明小模型（1.7B）可以在无金标准查询的情况下训练用于结构化查询生成，减少对大语言模型的依赖。
  2. 为金标准标注稀缺的领域特定KGQA提供了实用流水线。
  3. 凸显了执行反馈作为奖励信号的有效性，对您关于强化学习用于代码生成的工作具有参考价值。
