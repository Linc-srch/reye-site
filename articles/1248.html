CISA Adds Exploited Langflow and Trend Micro Apex One Vulnerabilities to KEV

CISA Adds Exploited Langflow and Trend Micro Apex One Vulnerabilities to KEV

Summary

The U.S. CISA added two actively exploited vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog: CVE-2025-34291 (CVSS 9.4) in Langflow, an origin validation error allowing arbitrary code execution and full system compromise, and CVE-2026-34926 (CVSS 6.7) in Trend Micro Apex One, a directory traversal flaw enabling malicious code injection. The Langflow bug has been exploited by Iranian state-sponsored group MuddyWater for initial access. Federal agencies must patch by June 4, 2026. These additions highlight the urgency of addressing both AI-related and legacy on-premise vulnerabilities under active attack.

摘要

美国CISA将两个被积极利用的漏洞加入其Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV)目录：Langflow中的CVE-2025-34291（CVSS 9.4，origin validation error导致任意代码执行）和Trend Micro Apex One中的CVE-2026-34926（CVSS 6.7，directory traversal漏洞）。Langflow漏洞已被伊朗国家支持黑客组织MuddyWater用于初始入侵。联邦机构须在2026年6月4日前完成修复。
