1) Why-read

ITGPT extends ITNet with GPT-like generative pretraining and SSL objectives for irregularly sampled multimodal timeseries, achieving SOTA without resampling or imputation.

2) CRGP

C — Context

Handling irregularly sampled multimodal timeseries is challenging in healthcare and predictive maintenance. Existing methods rely on resampling, imputation, or feature fusion, which are costly or lose information. Transformer-based SSL and GPT frameworks have succeeded in NLP but lack flexibility for irregular timeseries.

R — Related work

  • Kalman filters for linear state-space models with irregular data
  • RNNs with input space augmentation for unimodal irregular signals
  • Neural ODEs for continuous-time modeling (computationally expensive)
  • Kernel-based Gaussian processes (quadratic scaling)
  • ITNet for supervised regression on irregular multimodal timeseries

G — Gap

No existing model efficiently leverages unlabeled data for SSL or GPT-like pretraining on irregularly sampled multimodal timeseries. ITNet requires large labeled datasets and cannot predict future inputs, limiting its use in label-scarce scenarios.

P — Proposal

ITGPT extends ITNet by adding a GPT-like decoder that predicts future anchor data using causal cross-attention, enabling SSL and generative pretraining. The architecture uses separate encoder and decoder ITNet modules with shared weights, trained with both SSL and supervised objectives.

3) Figures

Figure extraction is performed separately and inlined post-generation.

4) Experiments

Main results

On TIHM healthcare dataset: ITGPT achieves SOTA with 0.85 F1 score (vs. 0.82 for best baseline). On CompX predictive maintenance: ITGPT achieves 0.91 accuracy (vs. 0.88 for best baseline). With only 10% labeled data, ITGPT with SSL pretraining achieves 0.80 F1 on TIHM, outperforming purely supervised ITNet at 0.72.

Ablation

Increasing model depth from 1 to 4 layers improves F1 from 0.82 to 0.85 on TIHM; mixing layers (cross-attention fusion) outperform simple concatenation by 3% F1.

Limitations

Only evaluated on two datasets; computational cost of cross-attention scales with number of timestamps; requires careful tuning of anchor timeline granularity.

5) Why it matters

  1. Directly addresses label scarcity in healthcare and predictive maintenance by leveraging unlabeled irregular timeseries.
  2. Eliminates need for resampling or imputation, preserving temporal fidelity.
  3. Provides a unified framework for SSL and GPT-style pretraining on multimodal irregular data, relevant to your work on robust time-series models.
