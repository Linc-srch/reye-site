How WeatherNext helped the National Hurricane Center better predict Hurricane Melissa’s historic landfall in Jamaica

How WeatherNext helped the National Hurricane Center better predict Hurricane Melissa’s historic landfall in Jamaica

Summary

In October 2025, Hurricane Melissa became the strongest hurricane on record to hit Jamaica. For the first time, the National Hurricane Center (NHC) predicted a storm would reach Category 5 intensity starting from Category 1 wind speed, aided by Google DeepMind's AI model WeatherNext. The model predicted rapid intensification and landfall five days in advance with high confidence, enabling early evacuations and resource mobilization. WeatherNext excels at both track and intensity prediction by training on decades of global weather data and specialized tropical cyclone datasets, running ensembles of 50 scenarios. This marks a historic breakthrough in anticipating extreme intensification events.

摘要

2025年10月，飓风Melissa成为有记录以来袭击牙买加的最强飓风。美国国家飓风中心（NHC）首次成功预测风暴从Category 1增强至Category 5，这得益于Google DeepMind的AI模型WeatherNext。该模型提前五天高置信度预测了快速增强和登陆，促使早期疏散和资源调配。WeatherNext通过训练数十年全球天气数据和热带气旋专用数据集，在路径和强度预测上均表现优异，并能运行50个情景的ensemble。这标志着预测极端增强事件的历史性突破。
