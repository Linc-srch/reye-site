System Safety

系统安全

Daily Overview

今日概览

Today's system safety research reveals a strong emphasis on robustness and reliability under distribution shift, with several papers addressing geometric collapse in multimodal LLMs (Safety Geometry Collapse, ReGap) and adaptive drift correction in RL (BAPR). A standout contribution is the identification of an 'Execution Wall' in TeleCom-Bench, where LLMs excel at linguistic tasks but fail catastrophically on procedural execution (~30% accuracy), highlighting a critical safety gap for industrial deployment. The IBAL framework for multi-agent RL introduces information-theoretic adversarial attacks that break inter-agent coordination, offering a new lens for stress-testing cooperative systems. Noteworthy announcements include the Argus deep research agent achieving 86.2% on BrowseComp, and the A-ProS multi-model feedback system for reliable autonomous programming. Recurring themes include contamination-aware evaluation (tax law reasoning), predictive prefetching for RAG latency reduction, and the use of LLM-guided tree search for disease forecasting. The field is increasingly focused on bridging the gap between linguistic competence and safe, executable action in real-world systems.
今日的系统安全研究高度关注分布偏移下的鲁棒性与可靠性，多篇论文探讨了多模态大语言模型中的几何塌缩（Safety Geometry Collapse, ReGap）以及强化学习中的自适应漂移校正（BAPR）。一项突出贡献是TeleCom-Bench中发现的“执行墙”（Execution Wall）：LLM在语言接口任务上准确率超过90%，但在程序执行任务（如解决方案生成）中骤降至约30%，凸显了工业部署的关键安全缺口。面向多智能体强化学习的IBAL框架引入了基于信息论的对抗攻击，破坏智能体间的协调，为压力测试合作系统提供了新视角。值得关注的公告包括：Argus深度研究智能体在BrowseComp上达到86.2%准确率，以及A-ProS多模型反馈系统实现可靠的自主编程。反复出现的主题包括：数据污染感知评估（税法推理）、面向RAG延迟降低的预测性预取（predictive prefetching），以及LLM引导的树搜索用于疾病预测。该领域正日益聚焦于弥合语言能力与安全可执行动作之间的鸿沟。
